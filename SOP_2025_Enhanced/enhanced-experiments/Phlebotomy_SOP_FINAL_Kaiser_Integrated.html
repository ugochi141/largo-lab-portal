1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) provides comprehensive guidelines for the safe and effective collection of blood specimens through venipuncture and capillary puncture procedures. This procedure encompasses patient preparation, specimen collection techniques, handling protocols, and quality assurance measures for all phlebotomy services.

Scope: This SOP applies to all phlebotomists, medical assistants, nurses, and other healthcare personnel authorized to perform blood collection within Kaiser Permanente healthcare facilities, including inpatient, outpatient, and point-of-care settings.

4. SAFETY PRECAUTIONS AND HAZARD COMMUNICATION

Standard Precautions:

  • Treat all blood and body fluids as potentially infectious
  • Hand hygiene before and after each patient contact
  • Use appropriate PPE: gloves, lab coat, eye protection
  • Safe needle handling and disposal practices
  • Immediate disposal of sharps in puncture-resistant containers
  • No recapping, bending, or breaking of needles

Bloodborne Pathogen Safety:

  • Hepatitis B vaccination for all personnel
  • Post-exposure prophylaxis protocols available
  • Immediate reporting of needlestick injuries
  • Annual bloodborne pathogen training
  • Engineering controls (safety needles, shields)

Patient Safety:

  • Positive patient identification using two identifiers
  • Assessment of patient condition and cooperation
  • Recognition and management of vasovagal reactions
  • Proper positioning to prevent falls
  • Monitoring for allergic reactions

7. SPECIMEN COLLECTION AND HANDLING

Patient Preparation:

  1. Patient Identification:
    • Use two independent identifiers (name, DOB, MRN)
    • Compare with requisition and patient wristband
    • Verbal confirmation from conscious patients
  2. Pre-collection Assessment:
    • Review test requirements and fasting status
    • Assess patient condition and cooperation
    • Check for allergies (latex, adhesives)
    • Identify preferred arm and previous difficulties
  3. Patient Positioning:
    • Seated or supine position preferred
    • Arm extended and supported
    • Ensure patient comfort and safety

Venipuncture Procedure:

  1. Site Selection:
    • Antecubital fossa (median cubital vein preferred)
    • Avoid areas with scars, tattoos, hematomas
    • Avoid sites with IV access or lymphedema
    • Consider alternative sites if necessary
  2. Preparation:
    • Apply tourniquet 3-4 inches above puncture site
    • Do not leave tourniquet on >1 minute
    • Clean site with alcohol in concentric circles
    • Allow to air dry (do not blow or fan)
  3. Collection:
    • Insert needle at 15-30 degree angle
    • Bevel up, smooth quick motion
    • Collect tubes in proper order
    • Fill tubes to required volume
    • Mix additive tubes immediately
  4. Completion:
    • Remove tourniquet before withdrawing needle
    • Apply gauze and pressure to site
    • Activate needle safety device
    • Dispose of needle in sharps container
    • Apply bandage after bleeding stops

Order of Draw for Multiple Tubes:

  1. Blood cultures (if ordered)
  2. Light blue (citrate) tubes
  3. Red or gold (serum) tubes
  4. Green (heparin) tubes
  5. Lavender (EDTA) tubes
  6. Gray (fluoride) tubes

8. ANALYTICAL PROCEDURE

Capillary Collection Procedure:

  1. Site Selection:
    • Adults: Lateral aspect of fingertip
    • Infants: Medial or lateral heel
    • Avoid areas with calluses or previous punctures
  2. Preparation:
    • Warm site if necessary (increase blood flow)
    • Clean with alcohol and allow to dry
    • Position extremity appropriately
  3. Collection:
    • Use appropriate lancet for age/site
    • Puncture perpendicular to skin surface
    • Wipe away first drop of blood
    • Collect blood without excessive squeezing
    • Fill containers to required volume

Special Collection Procedures:

  • Blood Cultures: Strict aseptic technique required
  • Glucose Tolerance Tests: Timed collections
  • Therapeutic Drug Monitoring: Peak and trough timing
  • Cold Agglutinins: Keep specimen at 37Â°C
  • Ammonia: Ice transport, immediate processing

Blood cultures 8-10 mL per bottle 10 mL per bottle Culture bottles

Critical Issues Requiring Immediate Action:

  • Patient identification errors: Stop procedure, verify identity
  • Severe vasovagal reaction: Medical assistance, monitor vital signs
  • Arterial puncture: Immediate pressure, monitor circulation
  • Needlestick injury: First aid, exposure reporting
  • Specimen contamination: Recollect if possible
