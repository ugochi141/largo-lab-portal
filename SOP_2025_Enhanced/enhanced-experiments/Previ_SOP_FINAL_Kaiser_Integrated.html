1. PURPOSE

This procedure describes the operation and maintenance of the bioMérieux PREVI® Color Gram automated staining system at Kaiser Permanente Largo Laboratory. This includes proper specimen preparation, slide staining procedures, quality control, and result interpretation for bacterial identification.

Why Gram Staining Is Important:

Gram staining is the most important and commonly performed microbiological staining technique. It provides critical information for:

  • Rapid preliminary identification of bacteria
  • Guiding empiric antibiotic therapy
  • Detection of polymicrobial infections
  • Assessment of specimen quality
  • Direct detection from clinical specimens

2. CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE

The Gram stain is a critical diagnostic tool that provides rapid, clinically actionable information about bacterial infections within minutes to hours, compared to 24-48 hours for culture results.

Clinical Applications

Clinical Condition Gram Stain Finding Clinical Action
Bacterial Meningitis Gram-positive diplococci (S. pneumoniae)
Gram-negative diplococci (N. meningitidis)		 Immediate targeted antibiotic therapy
Pneumonia Gram-positive cocci in chains (S. pneumoniae)
Gram-negative rods (H. influenzae)		 Empiric therapy adjustment
Urinary Tract Infection Gram-negative rods (E. coli, Klebsiella) Appropriate antibiotic selection
Septicemia Any bacteria in blood culture Critical value - immediate notification
Wound Infections Mixed flora or specific pathogens Surgical intervention decision

Impact on Patient Care

  • Rapid Results: 30-60 minutes vs 24-48 hours for culture
  • Mortality Reduction: Early appropriate therapy reduces mortality by up to 50% in sepsis
  • Cost Savings: Reduces unnecessary broad-spectrum antibiotic use
  • Antibiotic Stewardship: Promotes targeted therapy and reduces resistance

3. SCOPE

This procedure applies to Medical Laboratory Scientists (MLS), Medical Laboratory Technicians (MLT), and qualified laboratory personnel performing Gram stains using the PREVI® Color Gram system at Kaiser Permanente Largo Laboratory.

Specimen Types Processed

  • Blood cultures (positive bottles)
  • Body fluids (CSF, pleural, peritoneal, synovial)
  • Respiratory specimens (sputum, BAL, tracheal aspirates)
  • Wound and tissue specimens
  • Urine specimens
  • Genital specimens
  • Stool specimens (selected cases)

Personnel Requirements

Only trained and competency-assessed personnel may operate the PREVI® Color Gram system. Training includes:

  1. Initial training by qualified trainer (minimum 16 hours)
  2. Supervised practice (minimum 25 slides)
  3. Written competency assessment
  4. Practical competency demonstration
  5. Annual competency reassessment

6. SAFETY REQUIREMENTS

⚠️ Biosafety Level 2 Precautions

All specimens must be handled as potentially infectious. Follow BSL-2 practices and universal precautions at all times.

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

  • Laboratory coat (buttoned, long-sleeved)
  • Disposable gloves (double glove for high-risk specimens)
  • Safety glasses or face shield
  • N95 respirator when processing TB suspects
  • Closed-toe shoes

Biological Safety

Specimen Handling:

  • Process all aerosol-generating procedures in biosafety cabinet
  • Use sealed centrifuge cups for cytocentrifugation
  • Flame-fix slides away from drafts
  • Allow slides to completely dry before staining
  • Decontaminate work surfaces with 10% bleach

Chemical Safety

  • Crystal violet - potential carcinogen, avoid skin contact
  • Iodine - corrosive, store in dark container
  • Alcohol-acetone - flammable, use in well-ventilated area
  • Safranin - potential mutagen, handle with care
  • All reagents require SDS review before use

Exposure Response

  1. Needle stick/sharps injury - wash with soap and water
  2. Mucous membrane exposure - flush with water 15 minutes
  3. Report immediately to supervisor
  4. Complete incident report
  5. Follow post-exposure protocol
  6. Seek medical evaluation within 2 hours

10. MAINTENANCE

Daily Maintenance - PREVI® Color Gram

Start of Day Procedures:

  1. System Check:
    • Turn on PREVI® system
    • Check reagent levels (must be >10%)
    • Check waste container (<80% full)
    • Verify spray nozzles are clean
  2. Prime System:
    • Run prime cycle if idle >8 hours
    • Process takes 5 minutes
    • Ensures reagent lines are filled
  3. Quality Control:
    • Run QC slides before patient samples
    • Document results in QC log

Weekly Maintenance

Task Procedure Time Required
Clean Spray Nozzles Remove and soak in cleaning solution 30 minutes
Clean Slide Carriers Wash with mild detergent, air dry 20 minutes
Check Tubing Inspect for cracks or buildup 10 minutes
Clean External Surfaces Wipe with 70% alcohol 10 minutes

Monthly Maintenance

  • Replace reagent filters
  • Calibrate reagent pumps
  • Deep clean staining chamber
  • Update software if available
  • Review maintenance logs
  • Check spare parts inventory

⚠️ Important Maintenance Notes:

  • Never use bleach on metal components
  • Allow system to dry completely after cleaning
  • Document all maintenance in logbook
  • Report persistent issues to bioMérieux service

12. QUALITY CONTROL REQUIREMENTS

⚠️ CRITICAL QC RULE

NEVER report patient results if QC is not acceptable. Patient safety depends on accurate staining and interpretation.

QC Schedule

Frequency QC Required Action if Failed
Daily (each shift) • S. aureus (Gram-positive)
• E. coli (Gram-negative)		 Troubleshoot, repeat, do not report patients
New Reagent Lot All 4 QC organisms Verify with old lot before use
After Maintenance Both daily controls Repeat maintenance if needed
Weekly Additional organisms (S. pneumoniae, N. gonorrhoeae) Investigate trends

Acceptable QC Results

Evaluation Criteria:

  • Staining Quality: Clear differentiation between Gram-positive (purple) and Gram-negative (pink)
  • Background: Clean, no precipitate or debris
  • Cell Morphology: Intact cells with expected shape
  • Color Intensity: Appropriate contrast for interpretation

QC Failure Response

Immediate Actions for QC Failures

  1. STOP - Do not process patient specimens
  2. Investigate cause:
    • Check reagent expiration dates
    • Verify reagent levels adequate
    • Inspect spray nozzles for clogs
    • Review recent maintenance
  3. Corrective Actions:
    • Replace expired reagents
    • Clean spray nozzles
    • Run cleaning cycle
    • Prepare fresh QC slides
  4. Repeat QC after each action
  5. If still failing:
    • Use manual staining method
    • Contact bioMérieux service
    • Document in equipment log
  6. Documentation required:
    • QC failure details
    • Investigation findings
    • Corrective actions taken
    • Resolution time

13. TEST PROCEDURES

Slide Preparation

Direct Smear Preparation:

  1. Label slide: Patient name, MRN, date, specimen source
  2. Prepare smear:
    • Liquid specimens: 1 drop, spread to dime-size
    • Swabs: Roll across slide, not rub
    • Tissue: Touch prep or crush prep
    • Colonies: Emulsify in saline drop
  3. Air dry completely: 5-10 minutes or use slide warmer
  4. Heat fix: Pass through flame 3-4 times or 1 minute on 60°C warmer
  5. Cool before staining: 2 minutes minimum

Cytocentrifuge Preparation (Body Fluids)

  1. Mix specimen gently but thoroughly
  2. Load 0.5-1.0 mL into cytofunnel
  3. Centrifuge at 1000 rpm for 5 minutes
  4. Remove slide carefully to preserve cell button
  5. Air dry completely
  6. Fix with methanol for 1 minute
  7. Air dry before staining

PREVI® Color Gram Operation

Loading and Processing:

  1. System Preparation:
    • Verify system ready (green light)
    • Check reagent levels >10%
    • Scan operator ID badge
  2. Load Slides:
    • Place up to 30 slides in carrier
    • Ensure slides face correct direction
    • Scan slide barcodes if available
  3. Select Program:
    • Standard Gram: Most specimens
    • Enhanced: Thick smears
    • Gentle: Delicate specimens
  4. Start Cycle:
    • Press START button
    • Cycle time: 3.5 minutes per slide
    • System alerts when complete
  5. Remove Slides:
    • Allow to air dry (2-3 minutes)
    • Ready for microscopic examination

Manual Backup Method

When PREVI® is unavailable:

  1. Crystal violet - 1 minute
  2. Rinse with water
  3. Iodine - 1 minute
  4. Rinse with water
  5. Decolorize - 10-30 seconds (until runoff clear)
  6. Rinse immediately with water
  7. Safranin - 30 seconds
  8. Rinse with water
  9. Air dry or blot gently

14. CALCULATIONS AND RESULT REPORTING

Microscopic Examination

Systematic Review Process:

  1. Low Power (10x): Assess overall quality, distribution, background
  2. High Dry (40x): Identify areas of interest, WBCs, epithelial cells
  3. Oil Immersion (100x): Detailed morphology and arrangement
  4. Examine minimum 20 fields
  5. Report all morphologies seen

Reporting Format

Parameter Reporting Method Example
Bacteria Quantity Semi-quantitative per OIF Rare (1-5), Few (6-15), Moderate (16-30), Many (>30)
WBC Count Per HPF (400x) 0-5, 5-10, 10-25, >25/HPF
Epithelial Cells Per LPF (100x) Few, Moderate, Many
Morphology Descriptive Gram-positive cocci in chains

Standard Reporting Templates

Blood Culture:

"Gram-positive cocci in clusters seen. Many WBCs present. Preliminary: Possible Staphylococcus species."

CSF:

"No organisms seen. WBCs: 10-25/HPF, predominantly PMNs. RBCs: 0-5/HPF."

Respiratory:

"Mixed gram-positive and gram-negative flora. Many PMNs. Many epithelial cells present - suggest recollection."

17. LIMITATIONS AND INTERFERENCES

Method Limitations

  • Cannot detect: Mycoplasma, Chlamydia, Rickettsia, viruses, parasites
  • Poor staining: Mycobacteria, Legionella, some anaerobes
  • Antibiotic effect: May alter morphology or prevent detection
  • Dead organisms: May stain atypically
  • Specimen quality: Contamination affects interpretation

Technical Interferences

Problem Cause Effect Prevention
Over-decolorization Excessive alcohol exposure False gram-negative Strict timing, thicker smears
Under-decolorization Insufficient alcohol False gram-positive Ensure complete decolorization
Crystal violet precipitate Old reagent, contamination Artifacts, difficult reading Filter reagents, replace regularly
Thick smears Too much specimen Retained stain, poor morphology Proper smear preparation

Clinical Limitations

Important Considerations:

  • Gram stain is screening only - culture required for identification
  • Sensitivity varies by organism load (104 CFU/mL detection limit)
  • Cannot determine antibiotic susceptibility
  • May not correlate with culture results
  • Prior antibiotics may cause discordant results

Staining Quality Issues

Common Staining Problems

Crystal Violet Issues:

  • Precipitate formation - Filter or replace reagent
  • Weak staining - Check concentration and age
  • Contamination - Look for mold growth

Decolorizer Problems:

  • Too rapid - Dilute with water if needed
  • Too slow - Check alcohol concentration
  • Variable results - Ensure consistent timing

Safranin Counterstain:

  • Too light - Increase staining time
  • Too dark - Decrease time or dilute

19. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

PREVI® System Failure

Immediate Response to Complete System Failure

  1. Switch to manual staining immediately
  2. Notify:
    • Laboratory supervisor
    • All microbiology staff
    • Critical care units for STAT delays
  3. Prioritize:
    • Blood cultures
    • CSF specimens
    • OR specimens
    • ICU/ED STAT requests
  4. Contact bioMérieux service: 1-800-682-2666
  5. Document downtime in log

Reagent Shortage

  1. Check backup reagent inventory
  2. Borrow from nearby Kaiser facility
  3. Order STAT delivery from bioMérieux
  4. Use manual staining reagents if available
  5. Consider sending to reference lab

Biosafety Incidents

Spill Response - Infectious Material:

  1. Alert others and evacuate area
  2. Don appropriate PPE
  3. Cover spill with absorbent material
  4. Apply 10% bleach, wait 20 minutes
  5. Clean from outside to center
  6. Dispose in biohazard waste
  7. Decontaminate area again
  8. Complete incident report

Power Failure

  • PREVI® has 30-minute UPS backup
  • Complete current run if possible
  • Switch to manual staining
  • Store slides properly for later processing
  • Document affected specimens

24. DOWNTIME PROCEDURES

⚠️ PREVI® System Downtime Protocol

When automated staining is unavailable, implement manual backup procedures immediately.

Immediate Actions

  1. Switch to manual Gram stain method
  2. Notify all microbiology staff
  3. Post downtime notices
  4. Prioritize STAT and critical specimens
  5. Contact bioMérieux service
  6. Document downtime start

Manual Staining Capacity

Staff Available Slides/Hour Priority
1 technologist 20-30 Blood cultures, CSF only
2 technologists 40-60 Add body fluids, OR
3+ technologists 60+ All specimens

Recovery Procedures

  1. When system restored, run QC first
  2. Process backlog by priority
  3. Verify all reagent levels
  4. Document downtime end
  5. Calculate and report TAT impact
  6. Review incident for improvement
