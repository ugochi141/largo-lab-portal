1. PURPOSE

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) provides comprehensive instructions for the operation, maintenance, and quality control of the Cepheid GeneXpert System for molecular diagnostic testing in accordance with Kaiser Permanente Mid-Atlantic States Laboratory standards, ensuring compliance with CLIA, CAP, and Joint Commission requirements.

Key Objectives:
  • Standardize GeneXpert System operations across all testing locations
  • Ensure accurate detection of infectious agents and genetic markers
  • Maintain compliance with molecular diagnostic regulatory requirements
  • Support antimicrobial stewardship and infection control initiatives
  • Provide rapid results for critical clinical decisions
  • Integrate Kaiser Huddle recommendations for continuous improvement

  • GeneXpert testing supports rapid isolation decisions and treatment optimization
  • Critical for tuberculosis, MRSA screening, and multi-drug resistant organisms
  • Enables same-day therapeutic decisions for high-risk patients

2. CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE

The Cepheid GeneXpert System provides critical molecular diagnostic capabilities for detecting infectious agents, antibiotic resistance genes, and genetic mutations that directly impact patient care and public health.

Clinical Interpretation Guidelines

  • Positive results: Indicate presence of target nucleic acid; consider clinical context
  • Negative results: Do not exclude infection if pre-test probability is high
  • Invalid results: Require repeat testing; may indicate specimen quality issues
  • Resistance markers: Guide empiric therapy but require confirmation for definitive treatment

5. SAFETY REQUIREMENTS

‚ö†Ô∏è BIOSAFETY ALERT:

GeneXpert testing involves potentially highly infectious agents including M. tuberculosis, SARS-CoV-2, and multi-drug resistant organisms. Strict adherence to biosafety protocols is mandatory.

Biosafety Levels by Assay

Assay Biosafety Level Special Requirements
MTB/RIF Ultra BSL-3 practices N95 respirator, biosafety cabinet
SARS-CoV-2 BSL-2 enhanced N95 respirator recommended
MRSA/SA BSL-2 Standard PPE
C. difficile BSL-2 Contact precautions
CT/NG, GBS BSL-2 Standard PPE

Personal Protective Equipment Requirements

  • All testing: Lab coat, gloves, safety glasses
  • TB testing: N95 respirator (fit-tested), double gloves, disposable gown
  • COVID testing: N95 respirator, face shield, disposable gown
  • Sample preparation: Biosafety cabinet for TB and COVID specimens

Chemical Safety

  • Reagent cartridges contain guanidine thiocyanate (irritant)
  • Sample reagent contains sodium hydroxide (corrosive)
  • SDS available in laboratory and KP intranet
  • Spill kit location: Molecular Lab Cabinet M2
  • Emergency shower/eyewash: Room 305, North wall

Waste Management

  • Used cartridges: Biohazard waste (red bags)
  • TB cartridges: Autoclave before disposal
  • Sharps: Appropriate sharps containers only
  • Chemical waste: Follow KP hazardous waste protocol

QC Failure Protocol

‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL QC RULE

NEVER report patient results if QC is not acceptable. Patient safety depends on accurate results.

If QC fails:
  1. STOP - Do not report any patient results from affected module/instrument
  2. Investigate and document the failure
  3. Perform troubleshooting per manufacturer guidelines
  4. Rerun QC after corrective action
  5. If QC still fails, remove module/instrument from service
Follow-up Notification Requirements:
  1. Immediately notify Lead Technologist of QC failure
  2. Document all troubleshooting steps in QC log
  3. Contact Cepheid Technical Support (1-888-838-3222) and obtain case number
  4. Notify Technical Quality Specialist with:
    • Module/instrument affected
    • Troubleshooting performed
    • Cepheid case number
    • Current status
  5. Notify Laboratory Manager with complete documentation

CRITICAL: Patient results from the last acceptable QC may need review if systematic error is identified.

    • Thermal control

  • Action if fail: Module taken offline, service requested

Temperature Verification

  • Verified during annual preventive maintenance
  • Documented in service report

General Testing Procedure

Pre-Analytical Steps

  1. Verify specimen identification (two identifiers required)
  2. Check specimen acceptability against criteria
  3. Allow refrigerated cartridges to reach room temperature (minimum 30 minutes)
  4. Don appropriate PPE for specimen type
  5. For TB specimens, work in biosafety cabinet

Sample Preparation (varies by test)

MTB/RIF Ultra (Sputum):
  1. In BSC, add 2:1 ratio of Sample Reagent to sputum
  2. Vortex vigorously for 10 seconds
  3. Incubate 15 minutes at room temperature
  4. Vortex again for 10 seconds
MRSA/SA (Nasal swab):
  1. Insert swab into elution reagent vial
  2. Break swab at score line
  3. Cap and vortex for 10 seconds
C. difficile (Stool):
  1. Insert swab into unformed stool
  2. Transfer to sample reagent vial
  3. Vortex for 10 seconds

Analytical Steps

  1. Log into GeneXpert computer system
  2. Select "Create Test" from menu
  3. Scan or enter patient information
  4. Select appropriate assay
  5. Scan cartridge barcode
  6. Open cartridge lid and transfer specimen:
    • Use transfer pipette provided
    • Dispense slowly to avoid bubbles
    • Do not overfill chamber
  7. Close cartridge lid firmly
  8. Load cartridge into designated module within 30 minutes
  9. Close module door until it clicks
  10. Test starts automatically
  11. Monitor progress on screen

Post-Analytical Steps

  1. Review results when complete
  2. Verify internal controls passed
  3. Interpret results according to guidelines
  4. Release results in LIS
  5. Remove cartridge and dispose in biohazard waste
  6. For TB cartridges, autoclave before disposal
  7. Clean module door and work area
  8. Document any issues or comments

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Testing Notes

  • Never reuse cartridges
  • Process specimens within stability timeframes
  • TB specimens require BSL-3 practices
  • Critical values require immediate notification

17. LIMITATIONS AND INTERFERENCES

General Test Limitations

  • Detects nucleic acid, not viable organisms
  • Cannot determine if detected organism is causing current symptoms
  • Does not detect all possible pathogens
  • Negative results do not rule out infection
  • Not validated for asymptomatic screening (except where specified)
  • Results must be interpreted with clinical findings

Assay-Specific Limitations

Assay Specific Limitations
MTB/RIF Ultra ‚Ä¢ Does not differentiate MTB complex species
‚Ä¢ May detect non-viable organisms post-treatment
‚Ä¢ Some rare RIF mutations not detected
MRSA/SA ‚Ä¢ Does not detect all SCCmec types
‚Ä¢ Empty cassette variants may cause false positives
‚Ä¢ Screening test only, not for infection diagnosis
C. difficile ‚Ä¢ Detects gene, not active toxin production
‚Ä¢ May detect colonization
‚Ä¢ Not for test of cure
CT/NG ‚Ä¢ Not validated for pharyngeal specimens
‚Ä¢ Cross-reactivity with some Neisseria species
‚Ä¢ Not for test of cure <3 weeks post-treatment

Known Interferences

  • Blood: Large amounts may inhibit PCR
  • Mucus: Excessive mucus may cause invalid results
  • Medications: Recent antibiotics may cause false negatives
  • Transport delays: May degrade nucleic acid
  • Improper collection: Major cause of false negatives

Scenario 2: TB Specimen Spill/Exposure

Immediate Response:

  1. Alert all personnel - evacuate area if aerosol generated
  2. Close biosafety cabinet sash if involved
  3. Remove contaminated PPE carefully
  4. For skin exposure: wash with soap and water immediately
  5. For eye exposure: flush at eyewash station for 15 minutes

Decontamination:

  1. Don fresh PPE including N95 respirator
  2. Cover spill with absorbent material
  3. Apply tuberculocidal disinfectant liberally
  4. Allow 20-minute contact time
  5. Clean with disposable materials
  6. Re-disinfect area
  7. Dispose all materials as biohazard waste

Follow-up:

  • Report to Employee Health immediately
  • Complete incident report
  • Baseline TB testing if indicated
  • Document in exposure log

Scenario 3: Power Failure

  1. UPS battery backup maintains power for 30 minutes
  2. Save all work and properly shut down if extended outage expected
  3. Tests in progress will abort - will need repeat
  4. Transfer STAT specimens to MOB lab if available
  5. When power returns:
    • Restart all systems
    • Run Xpert Check on all instruments
    • Verify network connectivity
    • Check temperature logs for storage areas

Complete System Downtime Protocol

  1. Assessment Phase (0-15 minutes):
    • Determine scope of downtime
    • Identify affected instruments/modules
    • Contact Cepheid support if technical issue
    • Estimate downtime duration
  2. Communication (15-30 minutes):
    • Notify all stakeholders via overhead page
    • Send LIS broadcast message
    • Contact ED, ICU, and ID physicians
    • Post notices at specimen receiving
  3. Specimen Management:
    • Stop accepting new GeneXpert specimens
    • Store specimens appropriately:
      • TB specimens: 2-8¬∞C up to 7 days
      • MRSA swabs: Room temp up to 48 hours
      • C. diff: 2-8¬∞C up to 24 hours
    • Document all held specimens on downtime log

    Urgent MRSA Route to MOB Lab 2-3 hours
