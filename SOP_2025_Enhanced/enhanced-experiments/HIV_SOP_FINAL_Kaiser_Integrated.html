1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) provides comprehensive guidelines for HIV testing using current CDC-recommended algorithms including 4th generation HIV-1/2 antigen/antibody combination assays, HIV-1/2 antibody differentiation assays, and HIV-1 nucleic acid amplification tests (NATs). This procedure encompasses screening, confirmatory testing, and acute HIV infection detection.

Scope: This SOP applies to all laboratory personnel performing HIV testing using immunoassays, rapid tests, Western blot, and molecular methods within Kaiser Permanente laboratory facilities for screening, diagnosis, and monitoring of HIV infection in patients of all ages.

4. SAFETY PRECAUTIONS AND HAZARD COMMUNICATION

Biological Safety:

  • Treat all specimens as potentially infectious for HIV and other bloodborne pathogens
  • Use standard precautions for all blood and body fluids
  • Wear appropriate PPE: gloves, lab coat, safety glasses
  • Work in biological safety cabinet when required
  • Proper disposal of biohazardous waste
  • Hand hygiene before and after specimen handling
  • No mouth pipetting or eating in laboratory areas
  • Hepatitis B vaccination required for all personnel

Chemical Safety:

  • Review SDS for all reagents before use
  • Store reagents according to manufacturer specifications
  • Use fume hood for volatile substances
  • Emergency procedures readily available
  • Spill response materials accessible

Information Security:

  • Maintain strict confidentiality of all HIV test results
  • Secure storage of patient information
  • Limited access to HIV testing areas
  • Compliance with HIPAA regulations
  • Proper disposal of confidential documents

7. SPECIMEN COLLECTION AND HANDLING

Acceptable Specimens:

  • Serum: Red top or SST tubes (preferred)
  • Plasma: EDTA, heparin, or citrate tubes
  • Whole blood: For rapid testing
  • Oral fluid: For rapid testing (OraQuick)
  • Dried blood spots: For specialized testing

Collection Requirements:

  • Proper patient identification with confidentiality measures
  • Informed consent for HIV testing when required
  • Appropriate tube selection and labeling
  • Adequate sample volume (minimum 0.5 mL serum/plasma)
  • Room temperature transport acceptable
  • Refrigerate if testing delayed >4 hours

Special Handling Requirements:

  • Maintain chain of custody for forensic specimens
  • Heat inactivation may be required for some assays
  • Avoid repeated freeze-thaw cycles
  • Label with biohazard indicators
  • Secure transport to prevent exposure

Rejection Criteria:

  • Unlabeled or mislabeled specimens
  • Insufficient volume for testing
  • Hemolyzed specimens (for some assays)
  • Clotted plasma specimens
  • Specimens with visible contamination
  • Expired or improperly stored specimens

8. ANALYTICAL PROCEDURE

CDC Recommended HIV Testing Algorithm:

  1. Initial Screening:
    • 4th generation HIV-1/2 antigen/antibody combination immunoassay
    • Detects HIV-1 p24 antigen and HIV-1/2 antibodies
    • If negative: Report as HIV negative
    • If positive or equivocal: Proceed to step 2
  2. Supplemental Testing:
    • HIV-1/2 antibody differentiation immunoassay
    • Differentiates HIV-1 from HIV-2 antibodies
    • If positive for HIV-1 or HIV-2: Report as HIV positive
    • If negative or indeterminate: Proceed to step 3
  3. Nucleic Acid Testing:
    • HIV-1 RNA qualitative NAT
    • Detects acute HIV-1 infection
    • If positive: Acute HIV-1 infection
    • If negative: HIV negative or indeterminate

4th Generation Screening Procedure:

  1. Load specimens and controls onto automated platform
  2. System performs automated dilution and incubation
  3. Antigen-antibody capture and detection
  4. Signal measurement and interpretation
  5. Results calculated as signal-to-cutoff ratio

Rapid Testing Procedure:

  1. Verify test kit components and expiration
  2. Collect specimen according to manufacturer instructions
  3. Apply specimen to test device
  4. Add developer solution if required
  5. Read results at specified time intervals
  6. Interpret according to manufacturer's guide

11. RESULT INTERPRETATION AND REPORTING

Reporting Guidelines:

  • Negative: "HIV-1/2 antibody and HIV-1 antigen not detected"
  • Positive: "HIV antibody detected" or "HIV-1 RNA detected"
  • Indeterminate: Include recommendation for follow-up testing
  • Include testing methodology and limitations
  • Provide appropriate counseling referrals

Special Considerations:

  • Window period: Early infection may not be detected
  • Immunocompromised patients: May have atypical serologic patterns
  • Infants born to HIV+ mothers: Maternal antibodies present
  • Recent vaccination: May cause false positive results
  • Cross-reactivity: Other conditions may interfere

Reflexive Testing:

  • Automatic confirmatory testing for positive screening results
  • NAT testing for indeterminate differentiation results
  • Repeat testing for equivocal results
  • Additional testing for discrepant results
