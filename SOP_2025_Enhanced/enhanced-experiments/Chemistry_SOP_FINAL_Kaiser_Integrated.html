1. PURPOSE

This document describes the standard operating procedures for performing clinical chemistry testing at Kaiser Permanente Largo Laboratory using the Cobas® Pure Integrated Solutions analyzer. This SOP ensures accurate, reliable, and timely chemistry test results for patient care through standardized methodologies for all clinical chemistry testing including routine chemistry panels, electrolytes, metabolic markers, cardiac biomarkers, liver function tests, renal function assessments, therapeutic drug monitoring, and specialized chemistry assays.

Core Objectives:

  • Ensure accurate and precise chemistry test results
  • Maintain regulatory compliance with CLIA, CAP, and Joint Commission
  • Standardize testing procedures across all shifts and personnel
  • Support critical medical decision-making with reliable results
  • Minimize turnaround times for urgent patient care
  • Provide comprehensive metabolic and therapeutic monitoring

Individual Test Clinical Significance

Glucose

Clinical Significance: Primary marker for diabetes diagnosis and monitoring.

  • Increased: Diabetes mellitus, stress response, pancreatitis, Cushing's syndrome
  • Decreased: Insulin overdose, insulinoma, liver disease, adrenal insufficiency
  • Critical Values: <40 mg/dL or >500 mg/dL
  • Diagnostic Criteria: Fasting ≥126 mg/dL = Diabetes

Creatinine & eGFR

Clinical Significance: Most reliable markers of kidney function.

  • Increased Creatinine: Acute/chronic renal failure, dehydration, rhabdomyolysis
  • Decreased eGFR: Indicates reduced kidney function
  • Critical Value: Creatinine >10 mg/dL
  • CKD Stages: Based on eGFR values

Cardiac Markers

Troponin I: Gold standard biomarker for myocardial infarction.

  • Peak Time: 12-24 hours post-MI
  • Duration: Remains elevated 7-14 days
  • Critical: Any elevation above 99th percentile (>0.04 ng/mL)
  • Serial Testing: Repeat at 3-6 hours for trending

5. SAFETY REQUIREMENTS

⚠️ Universal Precautions

All patient specimens must be handled as potentially infectious. Follow standard precautions and bloodborne pathogen protocols at all times.

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

  • Laboratory coat (must be worn at all times in testing areas)
  • Disposable gloves (change between specimens and when contaminated)
  • Safety glasses or face shield when handling open specimens
  • Closed-toe shoes (no sandals or open-toe footwear)

Chemical Safety

Reagent Handling:

  • Review SDS for all reagents before use
  • Some reagents contain sodium azide (toxic)
  • Avoid contact with skin and mucous membranes
  • Do not pipette by mouth
  • Wash hands thoroughly after handling
  • Use fume hood for volatile reagents
  • Store chemicals according to compatibility

Biological Safety

  • Treat all specimens as potentially infectious
  • Clean spills immediately with 10% bleach solution
  • Dispose of all biological waste in appropriate biohazard containers
  • Report all exposures immediately to supervisor
  • Follow post-exposure protocol if needle stick or splash occurs

Electrical Safety

  • Do not operate equipment with wet hands
  • Report any frayed cords or electrical issues immediately
  • Ensure proper grounding of all equipment
  • Do not override safety interlocks

Spill Response Procedure

  1. Alert others in the area
  2. Put on appropriate PPE (gloves, gown, face protection)
  3. Cover spill with absorbent material
  4. Pour 10% bleach solution over the spill
  5. Allow 20 minutes contact time
  6. Clean up with disposable materials
  7. Dispose in biohazard waste
  8. Decontaminate area again with bleach
  9. Remove PPE and wash hands
  10. Document incident in log

Specimen Processing Requirements

Centrifugation Parameters:

  • Speed: 3000 rpm (1800 x g)
  • Time: 10 minutes
  • Temperature: Room temperature (20-25°C)
  • Balance tubes before centrifugation

Specimen Rejection Criteria

Reject specimens if ANY of the following conditions exist:

  • Hemolysis (H index >100) - affects K, LDH, AST, others
  • Lipemia (L index >300) - may require ultracentrifugation
  • Icterus (I index >20) - may interfere with some methods
  • Insufficient volume for ordered tests
  • Wrong tube type for requested test
  • Clotted heparin specimens
  • Unlabeled or mislabeled specimens
  • Specimens exceeding stability limits
  • Contaminated specimens (IV fluid, etc.)

9. MAINTENANCE

Daily Maintenance - Cobas® Pure Integrated Solutions

Maintenance Overview

The Cobas® Pure system includes:

  • Self-operating maintenance (SOM): Automatic scheduled actions without operator interaction
  • Software-controlled maintenance: System-performed actions (fully automatic or interactive)
  • Manual maintenance: Hands-on actions requiring operator interaction

Pre-Routine Daily Tasks:

  1. System Startup Check:
    • Check for splashes and spilled liquid on instrument surfaces
    • Check for leakage on syringes
    • Verify photometer check values are <1.4 Abs
    • Ensure ventilation caps on drain pipes are properly closed
  2. Run Daily Wash Rack:
    • Position 2: 600 µL of ISE Cleaning Solution/Activator
    • Position 3: 600 µL of SysClean
    • Prevents contamination of flow path and dilution vessel
    • Note: ISE calibration required after daily wash
  3. Check Reagent Status:
    • Review reagent levels and expiration dates
    • Check onboard stability
    • Load new reagents as needed
  4. Documentation:
    • Complete daily maintenance checklist
    • Document any issues in maintenance log

10. QUALITY CONTROL REQUIREMENTS

⚠️ CRITICAL QC RULE

NEVER report patient results if QC is not acceptable. Patient testing is prohibited when QC fails.

QC Schedule

QC Type Frequency Levels Acceptable Criteria
Daily QC Every 24 hours Level 1 & 2 Within ±2 SD
Third Party QC Monthly External control Within manufacturer range
Linearity Semi-annually 5 levels R² ≥0.99
Proficiency Testing 3x per year CAP samples 80% pass rate

Westgard Rules Applied

Multi-Rule QC System

The following Westgard rules are applied for QC evaluation:

  • 1-2s: Warning rule - One control exceeds 2 SD
  • 1-3s: Rejection rule - One control exceeds 3 SD
  • 2-2s: Rejection rule - Two consecutive controls exceed 2 SD on same side
  • R-4s: Rejection rule - Range between controls exceeds 4 SD
  • 4-1s: Rejection rule - Four consecutive controls exceed 1 SD on same side
  • 10x: Rejection rule - Ten consecutive controls on same side of mean

QC Failure Response

QC Failure Actions

  1. STOP all patient testing immediately
  2. Document failure in QC log with:
    • Date and time
    • Analyte(s) affected
    • QC lot number
    • Observed value vs expected range
  3. Troubleshooting steps:
    • Check QC expiration date and storage
    • Verify correct QC lot number in system
    • Repeat QC with fresh vial
    • Check reagent expiration and onboard stability
    • Review recent calibration status
    • Check for visible reagent contamination
  4. If QC still fails:
    • Recalibrate affected analyte(s)
    • Run QC again after calibration
    • If persistent, change reagent lot
    • Contact Lead Tech or Supervisor
  5. Document all corrective actions taken
  6. Do not resume patient testing until:
    • QC passes on two consecutive runs
    • Lead Tech approves resumption
    • Root cause is identified and resolved

11. CALIBRATION

Calibration Schedule

Analyte Frequency Calibrator Acceptance Criteria

Routine Chemistry Lot change or monthly CFAS Within ±5% of target
Enzymes Lot change or monthly CFAS Factor within range
Troponin Lot change Specific calibrator CV <10%

Calibration Procedure

  1. Prepare calibrator according to package insert
  2. Allow to equilibrate to room temperature (15 minutes)
  3. Mix gently by inversion (avoid foam)
  4. Load on analyzer within stability time
  5. Run calibration in duplicate
  6. Review calibration report for:
    • Factor within acceptable range
    • SD and CV acceptable
    • No error flags
  7. Run QC to verify calibration
  8. Document in calibration log

Calibration Verification

  • Required every 6 months or as needed
  • Use linearity materials spanning AMR
  • Results must be within ±10% of expected
  • Document all results and corrective actions

13. TEST PROCEDURE

Specimen Processing Workflow

Pre-Analytical Phase

  1. Specimen Receipt
    • Verify patient identification (2 identifiers)
    • Check specimen integrity
    • Note collection time
    • Prioritize STAT specimens
  2. Centrifugation
    • Balance tubes in centrifuge
    • Spin at 3000 rpm for 10 minutes
    • Check for complete separation
    • Note any HIL indices
  3. Aliquoting (if needed)
    • Use proper PPE
    • Label aliquots with patient ID
    • Avoid contamination between specimens
    • Store appropriately

Analytical Phase

  1. Loading Specimens
    • Place specimens in analyzer racks
    • Ensure barcode is readable
    • Load STAT specimens in priority positions
    • Start analysis
  2. During Analysis
    • Monitor for instrument alarms
    • Check for clots or bubbles
    • Observe HIL indices
    • Note any error flags
  3. Result Review
    • Check for critical values
    • Review delta checks
    • Verify auto-verification criteria
    • Investigate any flags

Special Procedures

Dilution Protocol

For results exceeding AMR:

  1. Dilute specimen with normal saline (not water)
  2. Common dilutions: 1:2, 1:5, 1:10
  3. Rerun diluted specimen
  4. Multiply result by dilution factor
  5. Document dilution in LIS
  6. Verify linearity of dilution

Reflex Testing

Automatic reflex tests triggered by:

  • Glucose >200 mg/dL → Add HbA1c
  • Elevated liver enzymes → Add GGT
  • Abnormal protein → Add protein electrophoresis
  • Elevated Troponin → Serial Troponins at 3, 6 hours

14. CALCULATIONS AND RESULT REPORTING

Common Calculations

Calculated Values

  • Anion Gap: Na - (Cl + CO2) [Normal: 8-16 mEq/L]
  • Osmolality (calculated): 2(Na) + Glucose/18 + BUN/2.8 [Normal: 275-295 mOsm/kg]
  • Corrected Calcium: Total Ca + 0.8(4.0 - Albumin) [if albumin <4.0]
  • A/G Ratio: Albumin/Globulin [Normal: 1.2-2.2]
  • BUN/Creatinine Ratio: BUN/Creatinine [Normal: 10-20:1]
  • LDL Cholesterol: Total Chol - HDL - (Trig/5) [if Trig <400]
  • eGFR: CKD-EPI or MDRD formula based on creatinine, age, sex, race

Result Reporting

Auto-Verification Criteria

Results meeting ALL criteria below are auto-released:

  • QC acceptable for the day
  • No instrument error flags
  • Results within reportable range
  • Delta check within acceptable limits
  • No critical values
  • HIL indices acceptable

Manual Review Required

Delta Check Criteria

Significant Changes Requiring Review

  • Glucose: >50 mg/dL or >50% change
  • Potassium: >1.0 mEq/L change
  • Sodium: >5 mEq/L change
  • Creatinine: >0.3 mg/dL or >50% change
  • Hemoglobin: >2 g/dL change
  • Platelet: >50% change

17. LIMITATIONS AND INTERFERENCES

Analytical Interferences

Interference Affected Tests Effect Action
Hemolysis K, LDH, AST, Mg Falsely elevated Recollect if H>100
Lipemia Most tests Light scattering Ultracentrifuge or dilute
Icterus Creatinine, cholesterol Spectral overlap Report with comment
EDTA contamination Calcium, Mg, Alk Phos Falsely decreased Recollect
Biotin Immunoassays False results Wait 8 hours post-dose

Physiological Variations

  • Diurnal variation: Cortisol, iron, glucose
  • Postural changes: Protein, hematocrit, calcium
  • Exercise: CK, LDH, AST, potassium
  • Diet: Glucose, triglycerides, BUN
  • Medications: Multiple analytes affected

Method Limitations

Important limitations to consider:

  • Troponin: May be elevated in renal failure without MI
  • Glucose: Ascorbic acid may cause false decrease
  • Bilirubin: Light sensitive, protect from light
  • Electrolytes: Pseudohyponatremia in severe lipemia
  • Proteins: Paraproteins may interfere with some methods
  • eGFR: Less accurate at extremes of muscle mass

18. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

Fire Emergency

  1. R.A.C.E. Protocol:
    • Rescue anyone in immediate danger
    • Alarm - Pull nearest fire alarm
    • Contain - Close doors to prevent spread
    • Evacuate or Extinguish if safe
  2. Secure patient samples if time permits
  3. Shut down analyzers if safe to do so
  4. Evacuate via nearest exit
  5. Assemble at designated meeting point

Chemical Spill

  1. Alert others in area
  2. Assess spill size and hazard
  3. Small spills (<100 mL):
    • Use spill kit
    • Wear appropriate PPE
    • Neutralize if required
    • Clean and dispose properly
  4. Large spills:
    • Evacuate area
    • Call Environmental Services
    • Notify supervisor
    • Complete incident report

Power Failure

  1. UPS maintains analyzer power for 30 minutes
  2. Complete current tests if possible
  3. Do not start new tests
  4. Store specimens appropriately:
    • Room temp stable: Leave at bench
    • Refrigerated: Move to backup refrigerator
    • Frozen: Keep freezer closed
  5. Switch to manual backup procedures
  6. Document all affected specimens

  • Precision: Within-run and between-run (20x2x2)
  • Linearity: Across analytical measurement range
  • Reference range verification: Minimum 20 healthy subjects
  • Interference studies: HIL, common drugs
  • Carryover: High to low concentration

  • Changes require Laboratory Director approval
  • Obsolete versions archived for 2 years minimum
  • All staff must read and acknowledge updates within 30 days
  • Training documented in personnel files

23. DOWNTIME PROCEDURES

⚠️ Instrument Downtime Protocol

When Cobas® Pure is unavailable, implement backup procedures immediately.

Immediate Actions

  1. Notify:
    • Laboratory supervisor
    • Emergency Department
    • ICU and critical care units
    • Nursing stations
  2. Post downtime notices in all areas
  3. Divert specimens to backup analyzer or reference lab
  4. Document downtime start in log

Backup Testing Options

Priority Tests Alternative Method TAT
STAT BMP, Troponin Point-of-care devices 15 minutes
Urgent CMP, Cardiac markers Send to main lab 2 hours
Routine All chemistry tests Batch and send out 24 hours

Point-of-Care Backup

Available POC devices:

  • i-STAT: BMP, lactate, blood gases
  • Glucometer: Glucose only
  • POC Troponin: Qualitative result

Note: POC results may not correlate exactly with main analyzer

LIS Downtime

Manual Processing

  • Use paper requisitions
  • Assign manual accession numbers
  • Record results on downtime logs
  • Call all STAT and critical results
  • Enter results when system restored

Recovery Procedures

  1. When instrument returns to service:
    • Run all startup procedures
    • Perform calibration if >4 hours down
    • Run QC (both levels)
    • Verify all QC acceptable
  2. Process backlogged specimens:
    • STAT first
    • Check stability of stored specimens
    • Reject any exceeding stability
  3. Notify all units that testing resumed
  4. Document:
    • Downtime duration
    • Number of specimens affected
    • Root cause if known
    • Corrective actions taken
