1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) provides comprehensive guidelines for the rapid detection of Group A Streptococcus (Streptococcus pyogenes) using immunochromatographic assays and other rapid testing methods. This procedure encompasses specimen collection, testing protocols, result interpretation, and quality assurance for the diagnosis of streptococcal pharyngitis.

Scope: This SOP applies to all healthcare personnel performing rapid strep testing using point-of-care devices in clinical laboratories, physician offices, urgent care centers, and emergency departments within Kaiser Permanente healthcare facilities.

4. SAFETY PRECAUTIONS AND HAZARD COMMUNICATION

Biological Safety:

  • Treat all throat specimens as potentially infectious
  • Use standard precautions for all patient specimens
  • Wear appropriate PPE: gloves, lab coat, eye protection if splashing possible
  • Proper disposal of biohazardous waste
  • Hand hygiene before and after specimen handling
  • No eating, drinking, or smoking in testing areas
  • Avoid aerosol generation during testing

Chemical Safety:

  • Review SDS for all reagents before use
  • Store reagents according to manufacturer specifications
  • Emergency procedures readily available
  • Spill response materials accessible

Patient Safety:

  • Proper patient identification procedures
  • Gentle specimen collection to minimize patient discomfort
  • Age-appropriate collection techniques
  • Communication with patients about procedure and results

7. SPECIMEN COLLECTION AND HANDLING

Patient Preparation:

  • Explain the procedure to patient/parent
  • Position patient appropriately (sitting with head tilted back)
  • Use adequate lighting to visualize throat
  • Avoid testing if patient has used mouthwash or eaten recently

Collection Technique:

  1. Throat Swab Collection:
    • Depress tongue with tongue depressor
    • Visualize tonsillar pillars and posterior pharynx
    • Insert sterile swab past teeth and tongue
    • Vigorously swab both tonsillar pillars and posterior pharynx
    • Avoid touching teeth, tongue, cheeks, or lips
    • Rotate swab to collect material from all surfaces
  2. Special Considerations:
    • Pediatric patients: May require restraint
    • Adults: Explain to minimize gagging
    • If exudate present: Include in collection
    • Multiple swabs: Use separate swabs for culture and rapid test

Specimen Handling:

  • Test immediately after collection for optimal results
  • If delay unavoidable: Store at room temperature up to 4 hours
  • Do not refrigerate specimens for rapid testing
  • If culture backup needed: Use separate swab in transport medium
  • Label specimens clearly with patient identification

Rejection Criteria:

  • Unlabeled specimens
  • Improper collection (saliva only, no pharyngeal material)
  • Contaminated swabs
  • Specimens >4 hours old without proper storage
  • Swabs in inappropriate transport medium

8. ANALYTICAL PROCEDURE

General Testing Protocol:

  1. Pre-test Preparation:
    • Verify test kit components and expiration dates
    • Bring all materials to room temperature
    • Check for damaged or leaking reagents
    • Prepare workspace and gather supplies
  2. Specimen Processing:
    • Add extraction reagent to specimen per manufacturer instructions
    • Mix thoroughly by rotating swab (typically 10 times)
    • Express liquid by squeezing tube sides
    • Wait specified time for extraction (varies by kit)
  3. Test Performance:
    • Apply specimen to test device as directed
    • Add specified number of drops
    • Start timer immediately
    • Do not move or disturb test device during incubation
  4. Result Reading:
    • Read results at exact time specified (typically 5-10 minutes)
    • Do not read results after maximum time limit
    • Interpret results according to manufacturer's guide
    • Document results immediately

10. REFERENCE RANGES AND CRITICAL VALUES

Result Interpretation:

Result Visual Appearance Clinical Interpretation Action Required
Positive Test line/window present Group A Strep detected Consider antibiotic treatment
Negative Only control line present Group A Strep not detected Consider backup culture if high clinical suspicion
Invalid No control line Test failure Repeat test with new device

Test Performance Characteristics:

Parameter Typical Range Clinical Impact Limitations
Sensitivity 85-95% May miss some infections False negatives possible
Specificity 95-99% Few false positives Cross-reactivity rare
Positive Predictive Value 90-98% Positive result likely true Depends on prevalence
Negative Predictive Value 95-98% Negative result usually reliable Consider backup culture

Critical Values and Actions:

  • Positive result: Group A Strep infection likely
  • Clinical correlation: Consider symptoms, age, season
  • Treatment decision: Based on clinical guidelines
  • Negative with high suspicion: Consider throat culture
  • Invalid result: Repeat test, consider alternative method

11. RESULT INTERPRETATION AND REPORTING

Reporting Guidelines:

  • Positive: "Group A Streptococcus DETECTED"
  • Negative: "Group A Streptococcus NOT DETECTED"
  • Invalid: "INVALID - Repeat testing required"
  • Include method used and limitations
  • Reference clinical guidelines for treatment

Clinical Correlations:

  • Consider patient age (more common in children 5-15 years)
  • Seasonal patterns (more common in winter/spring)
  • Clinical presentation (fever, exudate, adenopathy)
  • Centor criteria or McIsaac score
  • Local strep prevalence rates

Limitations and Comments:

  • Rapid tests may not detect all Group A Strep infections
  • Negative results do not rule out streptococcal infection
  • Consider throat culture for negative results with high clinical suspicion
  • Does not detect other bacterial pathogens
  • Not intended for screening asymptomatic individuals

Background color Excessive specimen, improper storage Use less specimen, check storage conditions Follow manufacturer instructions
