1. PURPOSE

This document defines the roles, responsibilities, and expectations for Lead Technologists at Kaiser Permanente Largo Laboratory. It establishes clear guidelines for leadership, technical expertise, administrative duties, and quality management to ensure consistent, high-quality laboratory operations across all shifts and departments.

Core Leadership Objectives

  • Provide technical expertise and mentorship to laboratory staff
  • Ensure compliance with all regulatory and accreditation standards
  • Maintain quality control and quality assurance programs
  • Facilitate effective communication between shifts and departments
  • Support continuous improvement initiatives
  • Foster a positive work environment and team culture

2. SCOPE

This procedure applies to:

  • All designated Lead Technologists across all laboratory departments
  • Charge Technologists serving in lead capacity
  • Senior Technologists with supervisory responsibilities
  • Technical Specialists with leadership roles
  • Acting Lead Technologists during coverage periods
Department Coverage: Chemistry, Hematology, Microbiology, Blood Bank, Molecular, POCT
Shift Coverage: Day (7A-3:30P), Evening (3P-11:30P), Night (11P-7:30A)

5. TECHNICAL RESPONSIBILITIES

Primary Technical Duties

  • Test Performance: Maintain competency in all department procedures
  • Troubleshooting: Resolve complex technical issues and instrument problems
  • Method Validation: Participate in new test implementation and validation
  • Quality Control: Review and approve QC, identify trends, implement corrections
  • Result Review: Verify critical values and complex result patterns
  • Technical Guidance: Provide expertise for difficult cases and specimens
  • Procedure Updates: Review and revise SOPs as needed
  • Equipment Oversight: Coordinate maintenance and calibrations

16. EMERGENCY RESPONSE LEADERSHIP

Emergency Response Duties

  • Downtime: Activate and coordinate downtime procedures
  • Evacuation: Ensure safe evacuation and accountability
  • Disaster: Implement disaster response protocols
  • Critical Staffing: Call in additional staff as needed
  • Communication: Serve as laboratory incident commander
  • Recovery: Coordinate return to normal operations
  • Documentation: Complete incident reports

Emergency Decision Authority

Situation Authority Level Actions Permitted
Analyzer Failure Full authority Send to backup location
Staffing Crisis Call in staff Approve overtime
Supply Shortage Emergency order Borrow from other sites
Safety Hazard Stop work Evacuate if needed
System Downtime Activate backup Implement manual procedures

Critical Reporting Requirements

Report Immediately to Supervisor:

  • Patient care impacts or delays
  • Significant quality issues
  • Safety incidents or exposures
  • Equipment failures affecting testing
  • Staffing shortages below minimum
  • Regulatory compliance concerns

Lead Technologist CAN:

  • ✓ Send tests to reference lab/backup location

  • ✓ Call in off-duty staff
