1. PURPOSE

This procedure establishes comprehensive protocols for maintaining laboratory operations during planned and unplanned downtime events. It ensures continuity of critical laboratory services, patient safety, and regulatory compliance during system failures, disasters, or maintenance periods.

Critical Objectives

  • Maintain essential laboratory testing capabilities during downtime
  • Ensure patient safety through continuous critical value monitoring
  • Minimize turnaround time delays and service disruptions
  • Preserve specimen integrity and chain of custody
  • Maintain regulatory compliance during emergency operations
  • Facilitate rapid recovery and return to normal operations

2. SCOPE

This procedure applies to all laboratory departments and personnel including:

  • Core Laboratory (Chemistry, Hematology, Coagulation, Urinalysis)
  • Microbiology and Molecular Diagnostics
  • Blood Bank and Transfusion Services
  • Point-of-Care Testing (POCT)
  • Specimen Processing and Phlebotomy
  • Laboratory Information Systems
  • Support Services (IT, Facilities, Biomedical Engineering)
24/7 Coverage: These procedures apply to all shifts including nights, weekends, and holidays
Activation Authority: Laboratory Supervisor or designated charge technologist

4. TYPES OF DOWNTIME EVENTS

Level 1: Complete System Failure

  • Total LIS failure affecting all departments
  • Hospital-wide power outage
  • Natural disaster or emergency evacuation
  • Complete network/internet failure
  • Response: Activate full emergency protocols

Level 2: Partial System Failure

  • Single analyzer or instrument down
  • Department-specific LIS issues
  • Partial power loss
  • Interface communication errors
  • Response: Implement targeted backup procedures

Level 3: Scheduled Maintenance

  • Planned LIS updates or upgrades
  • Scheduled analyzer maintenance
  • Building maintenance affecting utilities
  • Network maintenance windows
  • Response: Pre-planned alternative workflows

8. LABORATORY INFORMATION SYSTEM (LIS) DOWNTIME

Error Message Management During LIS Downtime

Error messages on instruments when running patients during LIS downtime are okay because:

  1. Expected behavior - The instruments are correctly detecting and reporting the loss of LIS connectivity, which confirms the system is working as designed
  2. Operations can continue - Most modern laboratory instruments can operate in standalone mode during LIS downtime, allowing patient testing to proceed despite the connection error
  3. Results are preserved - Instruments typically store results locally in their internal memory during downtime, preventing data loss
  4. Downtime procedures activated - The error message serves as a clear indicator to staff that downtime protocols should be followed (manual result recording, downtime logs, etc.)
  5. No actual malfunction - The error reflects a known, temporary communication issue rather than an instrument failure, so patient testing accuracy is not compromised
  6. Recovery is straightforward - Once LIS connectivity is restored, the stored results can usually be transmitted automatically or manually uploaded

High-Demand Laboratory Operations During Downtime

To maintain patient testing efficiency during LIS downtime:

  • Continue testing on all instruments despite error messages
  • Use manual logging to track all specimens tested
  • Print instrument results immediately after testing
  • Maintain chronological order of testing for proper recovery
  • Communicate delays to requesting departments
  • Prioritize STAT and urgent specimens
  • Follow established downtime workflows to minimize disruption

The error message serves as a clear indicator to staff that the LIS is unavailable, allowing them to follow established downtime procedures while maintaining testing capabilities. This ensures continuous patient care despite system limitations.

Downtime Protocol

  1. Pre-ordered specimens that were received in the lab before the computer system downtime:
    • Can be processed as normal
    • Results will be held in UDM until the LIS is up
    • Print results and call critical action values per lab policy
  2. Pre-order samples that were not received in the lab before the downtime:
    • Can be processed as normal if the order is in UDM
    • Place these samples in a holding rack, as they may need to be received after the system downtime
    • If there is no order in UDM proceed to step 3
  3. All samples received after the start of the computer downtime with no order on the UDM or with a downtime label:
    Will need to be ordered on the UDM before testing:
    • a. At the UDM select Search icon
    • b. Click edit
    • c. Scan or enter specimen number
    • d. Click the test selection, choose test to be analyzed
    • e. Click Save
    • f. Click OK
    • g. Click Save
    • h. Load sample on analyzer and perform the requested test
  4. Review results according to lab policy
  5. Print results from the UDM:
    • Call Critical Action values per lab policy
  6. Complete all testing for a specimen before proceeding to downtime recovery:
    • This includes selecting a rerun or performing microscopic
    • From UDM screen, click output on top right, then click Report (GP) to print the results
    • Click host output to send the results to LIS after everything comes back up
    • Note: Results must be validated to print

Simplified Downtime Specimen Labeling Procedures

Step 1: Order Retrieval and Preparation

  1. For NON-URGENT CARE patients: Lab reception staff will retrieve all orders from KPHC system
  2. For UC/CDU members testing: DOWNTIME ORDER FORMS for STAT Testing ONLY
  3. Label preparation: Specimen Processors need to prepare HAND WRITTEN CERNER DOWNTIME LABELS for each tube/sample if Cerner preprinted labels are not available for additional tubes/samples

Required Information on ALL Labels:

  • CERNER NUMBER
  • Patient's Name
  • Medical Record #
  • Test, collection time, initials

Step 2: Label Application (Choose appropriate method)

Method A - Primary tube (not shared/processed/aliquoted):

  1. Write test/test group and tube type on both large and small labels
  2. Attach the large label to the primary tube assuring the name and MRN of the patient are visible on the tube. If the patient's name and MRN are not visible, prepare HAND WRITTEN CERNER DOWNTIME LABELS for each tubes/samples if Cerner preprinted labels are not available for additional tubes/samples
  3. Initial the label
  4. Place a small label on the requisition beside the test or group of tests associated with the tube

Method B - Primary tube (for tests that must be spun, shared or split):

  1. Write the Patient's name, MRN, TEST, and tube type on the large label
  2. Write the test(s) name and tube type on the small labels
  3. Attach the small label to the primary tube
  4. Place a small label on the requisition next to the test or test group associated with that tube
  5. Example: CMP, MAG and SOSM (2 labels needed)
    • 1st label will only have CMP and MAG
    • 2nd label will only have SOSM
    • Both small labels will be placed on primary tube

Method C - Extra tubes:

  1. Place the "Extra tube" sticker on the tube
  2. Place the tube and a copy of the requisition in the bag
  3. Place the bag in the "Extra Tube Bin" or deliver it to the analyzing area

Extra Tube Label - Apply this sticker to extra tubes

Step 3: Sample Collection and Processing

  1. After sample collection:
    • STAT Samples: Along with completed worksheet goes directly to Lab Technologist for immediate processing and follow up
    • ROUTINE samples: Along with completed worksheet go to sample processor
  2. STAT Processing:
    • Lab Technologist will distribute samples to different benches as needed
    • Ensure all tests are performed, resulted and called/faxed by the performing technologist
    • Once all tests are completed, attach analyzer printouts to corresponding worksheets. Make 2 copies of the results, file one in the downtime file in alphabetical order by last name. Result worksheets can be faxed or tubed down to UC/CDU and other department. Store all samples with SAME CERNER NUMBER (One patient) together.
  3. ROUTINE Processing:
    • Sample processor will process all the samples, centrifuge them and store routines them appropriately and run the STATS on the instruments. All samples with SAME CERNER NUMBER (One patient) should be grouped together

Step 4: System Recovery

  1. When computer system is restored:
    • All orders need to be processed in CERNER with proper collection/receive time (Specimen Log-in)
    • Labels must be pulled
    • Sample processor with help of other phlebotomists on duty process, label and track each sample collected during downtime to RLR/Quest etc.
  2. Completed sample processing:
    • Lab Technologist processes all DONE samples
    • Pull labels and perform Specimen Log-in with actual collection/receive time
    • Result them in Cerner with help of other techs on duty
    • IMPORTANT: Process all patients specimens together. DO NOT SEPARATE ORDERS. All patient specimens must be processed together

Additional Downtime Steps

  • Extra Tubes: Place the "Extra tube" Sticker on the tube and file in downtime rack
  • When all tubes are labeled, highlight the test codes on requisition and double check for missed tests
  • Make one (1) copy of the Requisition for each label plus one
  • Place one (1) copy of the requisition, one (1) specimen tube, and all associated labels to the appropriate bench
  • Place the original requisition in the Processing alphabetical downtime file bin

Processing samples:

  1. Centrifuge samples as usual
  2. Check the information on the requisition against that on the primary tube and associated labels
  3. If all is correct, proceed to make aliquots following our normal processing procedure
    • Label appropriate processing containers with a large label. Initial and aliquot sample as usual
    • Place extra little labels
    • Deliver the specimens and a copy of the requisition for each test to the correct analyzing bench per lab protocol

Once the specimens are ran file in the downtime rack. Place the "Extra tube" Sticker on the tube and file in downtime rack

Once the computer system is up, all orders needs to be processed in CERNER with proper collection/receive time (Specimen Log-in) and all testing completed.

9. ANALYZER-SPECIFIC DOWNTIME

EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

When All Automated Systems Are Down

  1. Assess situation: Determine expected downtime
  2. Contact immediate support: Notify Laboratory Managers and Laboratory Directors with Service Reference number
  3. Prioritize samples: Process STAT and urgent samples first
  4. Use manual methods: Consider sending to reference lab or backup analyzer
  5. Document everything: Keep detailed records of all actions
  6. Call for help: Contact service representatives and managers
  7. Communicate delays: Inform requesting physicians of delays

Contamination Incidents

  1. Immediate containment: Isolate affected area
  2. Personal protection: Ensure staff safety with PPE
  3. Stop testing: Halt all operations in affected area
  4. Clean and disinfect: Use appropriate disinfectants
  5. Contact support: Notify Laboratory Managers and Laboratory Directors
  6. Document incident: Complete incident report
  7. Restart procedures: Only after thorough decontamination

Power Failure Response

  1. Secure samples: Ensure proper storage temperature
  2. Document time: Record exact time of power failure
  3. Check emergency power: Verify backup systems operational
  4. Preserve reagents: Maintain cold chain if possible
  5. Contact support: Notify Laboratory Managers and Laboratory Directors
  6. Recovery planning: Prepare for system restart procedures

10. POWER FAILURE PROCEDURES

IMMEDIATE POWER LOSS RESPONSE

  1. 0-30 Seconds: UPS systems maintain critical equipment
  2. 30 Seconds: Emergency generator activation (automatic)
  3. 1 Minute: Assess equipment on emergency power
  4. 5 Minutes: If no generator, begin equipment shutdown
  5. 10 Minutes: Activate full manual procedures
Equipment Emergency Power Battery Backup Action if No Power
Blood Bank Refrigerators Yes - Red outlets 4 hours Move to OR blood bank
Chemistry Analyzers Yes - Red outlets No Use POCT devices
Centrifuges Selected units No Manual backup available
Computers/LIS Yes - UPS 30 minutes Paper downtime
Specimen Storage Yes - Red outlets Passive cooling Ice/coolers available

Extended Power Failure (>4 Hours)

  • Transfer critical specimens to MOB Laboratory
  • Activate mutual aid agreements with nearby facilities
  • Consider specimen diversion to reference laboratory
  • Implement specimen collection restrictions
  • Deploy portable generators if available

11. NETWORK/INTERFACE DOWNTIME

Network Failure Impact Assessment

  • LIS Communication: No order receipt or result transmission
  • Analyzer Interfaces: Results not auto-verified or transmitted
  • POCT Connectivity: Results must be manually entered
  • Reference Lab: Cannot send electronic orders
  • EMR Access: Cannot view patient history or previous results

Interface Workarounds

  1. Manual Result Entry:
    • Print analyzer results every 30 minutes
    • Manually enter when system restored
    • Maintain paper log of all results
  2. Order Management:
    • Accept paper/faxed orders only
    • Create manual accession numbers
    • Queue orders for later entry
  3. Critical Communications:
    • Phone all critical values
    • Fax result reports to units
    • Hand-deliver STAT results

17. RECOVERY PROCEDURES

System Recovery Checklist

  1. Verify system functionality with IT
  2. Run system diagnostics and connectivity tests
  3. Perform QC on all analyzers before patient testing
  4. Clear any analyzer error messages or flags
  5. Verify all interfaces are communicating
  6. Process backlogged STAT specimens first
  7. Enter all manual results into LIS
  8. Match downtime specimens to electronic orders
  9. Release held results after verification
  10. Notify all units that normal operations resumed
  11. Complete post-downtime report
  12. Schedule debriefing meeting

Result Entry Recovery Process

  1. Preparation Phase:
    • Gather all downtime documentation
    • Sort by priority and time received
    • Assign entry teams if volume is high
  2. Entry Phase:
    • Enter critical results first
    • Match downtime numbers to patient orders
    • Enter results with original collection time
    • Add comment "Result delayed due to downtime"
  3. Verification Phase:
    • Double-check all critical value entries
    • Verify patient identification matches
    • Confirm all results entered
    • Run reports to identify any gaps

Appendix B: Decision Tree - Downtime Response

Duration Unknown? Assume >4 hours, activate full protocol

<30 minutes? Continue with manual backup, hold routine

30 min - 2 hours? Process STAT only, notify units

2-4 hours? Send STAT to MOB, batch routine

>4 hours? Full MOB diversion, reference lab activation

Appendix C: Never Events During Downtime

  • Never skip patient identification verification
  • Never discard specimens without processing
  • Never delay critical value notification
  • Never bypass QC requirements
  • Never guess at results or patient information
  • Never work beyond your competency level
