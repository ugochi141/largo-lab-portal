1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) provides comprehensive guidelines for medical toxicology testing including drugs of abuse screening, therapeutic drug monitoring, and toxic substance identification. This procedure encompasses immunoassay screening, confirmatory testing by chromatographic methods, and forensic toxicology applications.

Scope: This SOP applies to all laboratory personnel performing toxicology testing using immunoassays, LC-MS/MS, GC-MS, and other analytical platforms within Kaiser Permanente laboratory facilities for clinical, workplace, and forensic applications.

2. REGULATORY REQUIREMENTS AND COMPLIANCE

Regulatory Standards:

  • CLIA '88: High complexity testing requirements
  • CAP: Laboratory Accreditation Program standards
  • FDA: Device regulations and cleared assays only
  • DEA: Controlled substance regulations
  • SAMHSA: Workplace drug testing guidelines
  • Joint Commission: Patient safety standards
  • OSHA: Laboratory safety requirements
  • Kaiser Permanente: Quality management system requirements
  • Chain of Custody: Federal workplace testing requirements

3. PERSONNEL QUALIFICATIONS AND COMPETENCY

Required Qualifications:

  • Medical Technologist (MT) or Medical Laboratory Scientist (MLS)
  • Bachelor's degree in Clinical Laboratory Science, Chemistry, or related field
  • ASCP certification (MT, MLS) or equivalent
  • Additional certification in toxicology preferred (e.g., ABFT)
  • Completion of Documents 14952_0 and 13792_0 competency assessments
  • Chain of custody training certification

Competency Requirements:

  • Initial competency assessment before independent testing
  • Annual competency verification
  • Proficiency testing participation
  • Method-specific training for each platform
  • Understanding of pharmacokinetics and toxicokinetics
  • Legal and ethical considerations in toxicology testing

4. SAFETY PRECAUTIONS AND HAZARD COMMUNICATION

Biological Safety:

  • Treat all specimens as potentially infectious
  • Use standard precautions for blood and body fluids
  • Wear appropriate PPE: gloves, lab coat, safety glasses
  • Work in chemical fume hood when required
  • Proper disposal of biohazardous waste
  • Special handling for forensic specimens

Chemical Safety:

  • Review SDS for all reagents and solvents
  • Store controlled substances per DEA requirements
  • Use fume hood for volatile organic solvents
  • Emergency procedures readily available
  • Spill response kits accessible
  • Fire suppression systems verified

Controlled Substance Security:

  • Secure storage for DEA controlled substances
  • Access control and key management
  • Inventory tracking and documentation
  • Disposal procedures for expired materials

7. SPECIMEN COLLECTION AND HANDLING

Acceptable Specimens:

  • Urine: Most common for drug screening
  • Serum/Plasma: Therapeutic drug monitoring
  • Whole blood: Forensic applications
  • Oral fluid: Alternative matrix testing
  • Hair: Long-term detection window

Collection Requirements:

  • Chain of custody documentation for workplace testing
  • Proper patient/donor identification
  • Temperature measurement for urine specimens
  • Appropriate containers and preservatives
  • Collection site security for forensic specimens
  • Minimum volumes: Urine 30mL, Serum 2mL

Chain of Custody Procedures:

  1. Collector verifies donor identity
  2. Specimen collected under direct observation if required
  3. Specimen sealed and labeled in donor's presence
  4. Donor and collector sign custody form
  5. Secure transport to laboratory
  6. Laboratory receives and documents custody transfer

Rejection Criteria:

  • Broken chain of custody
  • Insufficient volume
  • Improper container or labeling
  • Temperature out of range (urine: 90-100°F)
  • Signs of adulteration or substitution
  • Specimen integrity compromised

8. ANALYTICAL PROCEDURE

Screening Methods (Immunoassay):

Drug Class Cutoff (ng/mL) Method Confirmation Required
Amphetamines 1000 EMIT/FPIA Yes
Cannabinoids (THC) 50 EMIT/FPIA Yes
Cocaine 300 EMIT/FPIA Yes
Opiates 2000 EMIT/FPIA Yes
Phencyclidine (PCP) 25 EMIT/FPIA Yes

Confirmation Methods (GC-MS/LC-MS/MS):

  1. Sample preparation and extraction
  2. Chromatographic separation
  3. Mass spectrometric detection
  4. Quantitative analysis against calibration curve
  5. Quality control sample analysis
  6. Data review and interpretation

Specimen Validity Testing:

  • Creatinine: ≥20 mg/dL
  • Specific gravity: 1.003-1.020
  • pH: 4.5-8.0
  • Adulterants: Oxidants, glutaraldehyde, nitrites

9. QUALITY CONTROL

QC Requirements:

Test Method QC Frequency QC Materials Acceptable Range
Immunoassay Screening Each run Negative/Positive controls ±20% of target value
GC-MS Confirmation Each batch 3 levels of controls ±20% of target value
LC-MS/MS Each batch 3 levels of controls ±15% of target value
Validity Testing Each run Normal/Abnormal controls ±2 SD from mean

Proficiency Testing:

  • External PT samples analyzed quarterly
  • Blind QC samples throughout testing
  • Inter-laboratory comparisons
  • Method comparison studies

10. REFERENCE RANGES AND CRITICAL VALUES

Therapeutic Drug Monitoring Ranges:

Drug Therapeutic Range Toxic Level Units
Digoxin 0.8-2.0 >2.5 ng/mL
Lithium 0.6-1.2 >1.5 mEq/L
Phenytoin 10-20 >30 μg/mL
Theophylline 5-15 >20 μg/mL
Valproic acid 50-100 >150 μg/mL

Critical Values:

  • Toxic drug levels: Above therapeutic range
  • Positive workplace testing: Above cutoff concentrations
  • Life-threatening toxicity: Methanol, ethylene glycol
  • Notification time: Within 1 hour for critical values
  • Chain of custody: Maintain for all workplace specimens

11. RESULT INTERPRETATION AND REPORTING

Reporting Categories:

  • Negative: Below cutoff or not detected
  • Positive: Above cutoff with confirmation
  • Invalid: Specimen integrity compromised
  • Adulterated: Presence of interfering substances
  • Substituted: Not consistent with human urine
  • Dilute: Creatinine 2-20 mg/dL

Workplace Testing Results:

  • Medical Review Officer (MRO) receives all results
  • Positive results require MRO interview
  • Chain of custody maintained throughout process
  • Secure transmission of results

18. PROFICIENCY TESTING

PT Program Requirements:

  • Provider: CAP, AACC, or other approved provider
  • Frequency: 3 times per year minimum
  • Analytes: All routinely tested drugs and metabolites
  • Acceptable Performance: ±20% or ±2 SD (whichever is greater)

PT Failure Response:

  1. Immediate investigation of root cause
  2. Review of patient results since last successful PT
  3. Method evaluation and corrective action
  4. Retesting if required by PT provider
  5. Documentation of all actions taken
  6. Regulatory notification if required
