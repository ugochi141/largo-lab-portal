1. PURPOSE

This document describes the standard operating procedures for performing hematology testing at Kaiser Permanente Largo Laboratory using the Sysmex XN-3000 and XN-9000 hematology analyzers. This SOP ensures accurate, reliable, and timely hematology test results for patient care through standardized methodologies for all hematological testing including complete blood count (CBC), differential analysis, reticulocyte counting, body fluid analysis, morphology review, and specialized hematological studies.

Core Objectives:

  • Ensure accurate and precise hematology test results
  • Maintain regulatory compliance with CLIA, CAP, and Joint Commission
  • Standardize testing procedures across all shifts and personnel
  • Support critical medical decision-making with reliable results
  • Minimize turnaround times for urgent patient care
  • Provide comprehensive hematological monitoring and morphology review

Individual Parameter Clinical Significance

Hemoglobin & Hematocrit

Clinical Significance: Primary indicators of oxygen-carrying capacity and anemia.

  • Decreased: Iron deficiency, chronic disease, blood loss, hemolysis
  • Increased: Dehydration, polycythemia, chronic hypoxia
  • Critical Values: Hgb <6.0 g/dL or >20.0 g/dL
  • Monitoring: Response to treatment, blood loss, chronic conditions

White Blood Cell Count & Differential

Clinical Significance: Primary indicator of immune system function and infection.

  • Increased WBC: Bacterial infection, leukemia, inflammation, stress
  • Decreased WBC: Viral infection, chemotherapy, aplastic anemia
  • Left Shift: Increased immature neutrophils indicating bacterial infection
  • Critical Value: WBC <1.0 or >50.0 × 10³/μL

Platelet Count

Clinical Significance: Essential for primary hemostasis and bleeding assessment.

  • Thrombocytopenia: <150 × 10³/μL - increased bleeding risk
  • Thrombocytosis: >450 × 10³/μL - possible thrombotic risk
  • Critical Value: <20 × 10³/μL or >1000 × 10³/μL
  • Monitoring: Chemotherapy, anticoagulation, surgical bleeding

4. TEST PRINCIPLES AND METHODOLOGIES

Sysmex XN Technology Overview

Core Technologies

  • Hydrodynamic Focusing (DC Detection): Precise cell counting for RBC and PLT using electrical impedance
  • Flow Cytometry with Semiconductor Laser: 633nm laser for WBC differential and cell characterization
  • SLS-Hemoglobin Method: Sodium Lauryl Sulfate cyanide-free hemoglobin measurement at 555nm
  • Fluorescence Flow Cytometry: Side scatter, forward scatter, and fluorescence intensity for cell differentiation

Analysis Channels

Channel Parameters Method Reagents
WNR WBC, BASO DC detection + lysing CELLPACK DCL, SULFOLYSER
WDF WBC Diff (5-part) Fluorescence flow cytometry CELLPACK DST, Lysercell WDF, Fluorocell WDF
WPC Abnormal cells, blasts Fluorescence flow cytometry CELLPACK DST, Lysercell WPC, Fluorocell WPC
RET Reticulocytes, IRF Fluorescence flow cytometry CELLPACK DFL, CELLSHEATH, Fluorocell RET
PLT-F Platelet optical, IPF Fluorescence flow cytometry CELLPACK DFL, Fluorocell PLT

Directly Measured Parameters

  • WBC, RBC, HGB, HCT, RDW-SD, PLT-I, PLT-F
  • NEUT%, LYMP%, MONO%, EOS%, BASO%, IG%
  • NRBC#, RET%, IRF, IPF%

Calculated Parameters

  • MCV = HCT × 10 / RBC
  • MCH = HGB × 10 / RBC
  • MCHC = HGB × 100 / HCT
  • RDW-CV = RDW-SD / MCV × 100
  • Absolute counts = WBC × differential %

Unacceptable Specimens per Kaiser Protocol

  • Clotted specimens: Check all EDTA tubes with applicator sticks before analysis
  • Wrong anticoagulant: Heparin causes WBC clumping, citrate dilutes counts by 10%
  • Hemolyzed specimen: Falsely decreases RBC count and hematocrit
  • Specimen >24 hours at RT: WBC degradation, platelet swelling
  • Insufficient volume: Excess anticoagulant causes cell shrinkage
  • Unlabeled specimens: Two patient identifiers required
  • IV contamination: Dilutional effect on all parameters
  • Lipemic samples: Turbidity interferes with HGB measurement
  • Cold agglutinins: Warm to 37°C before analysis

Special Collection Considerations

  • EDTA-dependent platelet clumping: Collect sodium citrate tube, multiply PLT by 1.1
  • Refrigerated samples: Allow to reach room temperature (15-25°C) before analysis
  • Mixing requirement: 8-10 gentle inversions, DO NOT use mechanical rocker
  • Pediatric draws: Minimum 160μL for microtube analysis mode

10. QUALITY CONTROL REQUIREMENTS

Kaiser Permanente XN QC Requirements

  • Frequency: Three levels of XN CHECK every 8 hours and with each new shift
  • Acceptance: All parameters within ±2 SD (manufacturer's ranges)
  • Action for failure: Do not report patient results until resolved
  • Documentation: Auto-logged in IPU, export monthly for permanent record
  • Moving averages: Monitor Bull's algorithm for MCV, MCH, MCHC (±2% daily variation)

QC Procedure

  1. Preparation:
    • Remove XN CHECK from refrigerator, allow 15 minutes to reach room temperature
    • Check expiration date and open stability (7 days)
    • Mix by gentle inversion 8-10 times (do not vortex)
  2. Analysis:
    • Select QC mode on analyzer
    • Run Level 1 (Low), Level 2 (Normal), Level 3 (High) in sequence
    • Verify all parameters pass before proceeding
  3. Troubleshooting QC Failures:
    • Repeat failed level once
    • If still fails, check reagent levels and expiration
    • Perform maintenance if indicated
    • Run fresh QC vial if suspect deterioration
    • Contact supervisor if unresolved

12. TROUBLESHOOTING

11.1 System Startup Procedure

  1. Power On Sequence:
    • Turn on IPU and monitor first
    • Power on XN analyzer modules
    • Launch Extended IPU software
    • Wait for READY status (green indicator)
  2. Startup Checks:
    • Verify reagent levels >10%
    • Check waste container <80% full
    • Confirm temperature 18-30°C
    • Run background counts
  3. QC Protocol:
    • Run 3 levels of XN CHECK
    • Verify all parameters pass
    • Document in QC log

11.2 CBC with Differential - Sampler Mode

  1. Pre-analytical:
    • Verify specimen identification - two identifiers
    • Check specimen quality (no clots, proper fill)
    • Mix specimen by inversion 8-10 times
    • Note any visible abnormalities (lipemia, icterus)
  2. Rack Loading:
    • Place specimens in 10-position racks
    • Ensure tubes are properly seated
    • Load rack in right sampler area
    • Analysis auto-starts when rack detected
  3. Analysis Process:
    • Automatic barcode reading
    • Sample aspiration: 200μL for CBC+Diff
    • Analysis time: ~60 seconds per sample
    • Auto-rerun based on IPU rules
  4. IPU Rules Engine Actions:
    • WBC <2.0 or >30.0: Auto-reflex to manual diff
    • PLT <50 or >1000: Auto-reflex to PLT-F
    • Blast flag: Auto-reflex to WPC channel
    • NRBC present: Auto-correction of WBC

11.3 Manual Mode Analysis

  1. When to Use Manual Mode:
    • Single STAT specimens
    • Micro-volume samples
    • Body fluids
    • Troubleshooting failed samples
  2. Procedure:
    • Select Manual mode on screen
    • Choose analysis profile (Whole Blood, Low WBC, Pre-dilute)
    • Enter or scan specimen ID
    • Mix specimen thoroughly
    • Open cap carefully (biohazard precaution)
    • Present to manual sample probe
    • Press start button when prompted
  3. Volume Requirements:
    • Closed tube mode: 1000μL minimum
    • Open tube mode: 300μL
    • Capillary mode: 160μL

11.4 Blood Smear Review Criteria

Mandatory Smear Review (Kaiser Permanente Criteria)

  • WBC <2.0 or >30.0 × 10³/μL
  • Platelet <50 or >1000 × 10³/μL
  • Hemoglobin <7.0 or >20.0 g/dL
  • Any instrument flag for abnormal cells
  • Blast flag, Atypical Lymph flag, Left Shift flag
  • NRBC >1%
  • First-time patient with any abnormality
  • Delta check failure (>20% change)
  • Physician request for morphology

Manual Differential Procedure

  1. Smear Preparation:
    • Use SP-50 automated slide maker when available
    • Manual wedge technique for backup
    • Stain with Wright-Giemsa per protocol
    • Verify staining quality before review
  2. Microscopic Examination:
    • 10× objective: Overall cellularity, stain quality
    • 40× objective: WBC estimate, RBC morphology scan
    • 100× oil: Count 100 WBCs for differential
    • Document all morphologic abnormalities
  3. Reporting Guidelines:
    • Report WBC differential as percentages
    • Grade RBC morphology 1+ to 4+
    • Estimate platelets: 5-10 per oil field = adequate
    • Note any inclusions, parasites, abnormal cells

11.5 Body Fluid Analysis

  1. Specimen Processing:
    • CSF: Process immediately, no anticoagulant
    • Serous fluids: EDTA anticoagulant required
    • Synovial: EDTA, may need hyaluronidase treatment
  2. XN Body Fluid Mode:
    • Select BF mode in software
    • Minimum volume: 300μL
    • Reports: WBC-BF, RBC-BF, MN%, PMN%
    • TC-BF# (nucleated cells)
  3. Manual Backup Method:
    • Hemocytometer counting for low counts
    • Cytospin for differential
    • Follow CAP guidelines for cell counting

11.6 Reticulocyte Analysis

  1. Automatically performed with CBC when ordered
  2. RET channel uses fluorescent RNA staining
  3. Reports: RET%, RET#, IRF (Immature Reticulocyte Fraction)
  4. Reference range: 0.5-2.0% (adults)
  5. Clinical use: Anemia evaluation, chemotherapy monitoring

15. REFERENCE RANGES

Critical Limitations

  • Cannot differentiate all abnormal cell types - manual review required
  • Cannot identify all RBC inclusions (Howell-Jolly, basophilic stippling)
  • May not detect low-level parasitemia
  • Limited accuracy with severe pancytopenia
  • Automated diff unreliable with WBC <0.5 or >100 × 10³/μL

XN-9000 (XN-1000 × 2 configuration)

  1. Place 2 tubes of CELLCLEAN AUTO in rack positions 9 and 10

21. QUALITY ASSURANCE

Biological Safety - Universal Precautions

  • All specimens are potentially infectious - handle with standard precautions
  • PPE required: Lab coat, gloves, safety glasses when uncapping
  • Face shield required for specimen processing with splash risk
  • No eating, drinking, or cosmetics application in testing areas
  • Wash hands after glove removal and before leaving lab

Exposure Protocol

  1. Needlestick/Cut:
    • Wash with soap and water immediately
    • Report to supervisor within 30 minutes
    • Complete incident report
    • Report to Employee Health for evaluation
  2. Splash to Eyes/Mucous Membranes:
    • Flush with water for 15 minutes
    • Use eyewash station
    • Report and seek medical evaluation

Chemical Safety

Reagent Hazard Precautions
SULFOLYSER Corrosive Avoid skin contact, use gloves
Wright-Giemsa Methanol (flammable) Use in ventilated area, no flames
CELLCLEAN Sodium hypochlorite Avoid mixing with acids
Fluorocell reagents Contains sodium azide Do not pour down copper drains

Waste Disposal

  • Biohazard waste: Red bags for contaminated materials
  • Sharps: Puncture-resistant containers
  • Chemical waste: Follow SDS guidelines
  • Analyzer waste: Treat as biohazard, dispose per protocol

23. DOWNTIME PROCEDURES

Analyzer Failure - Immediate Response

  1. Assessment (First 15 minutes):
    • Determine scope of failure (single module vs entire system)
    • Check for simple fixes (power, connections, reagents)
    • Estimate downtime duration
    • Notify supervisor and affected units
  2. Activation of Backup (15-30 minutes):
    • Switch STAT work to XN-3000 backup analyzer
    • If both systems down, activate manual backup
    • Notify MOB laboratory for overflow: 301-555-2000
    • Implement paper log for specimen tracking
  3. Communication:
    • Send notification via secure messaging to all providers
    • Post signs at specimen drop-off locations
    • Update laboratory phone greeting
    • Estimated TAT: Add 2 hours to routine

Manual Backup Procedures

Test Manual Method Time Limitations
Hematocrit Microhematocrit centrifuge 5 min No MCV, MCH, MCHC
Hemoglobin HemoCue or spectrophotometer 2 min Limited throughput
WBC Count Hemocytometer with dilution 15 min No differential
Platelet Count Hemocytometer or smear estimate 10 min Less precise
Differential Manual 100-cell count on smear 10 min Labor intensive

Contingency Routing

  • STAT specimens: Process manually or send to MOB lab
  • Routine CBCs: Batch and hold for analyzer repair (if <4 hours)
  • Oncology patients: Priority manual processing
  • Pre-op cases: HemoCue for hemoglobin only if urgent
  • Reference lab: Send stable specimens if downtime >24 hours

Recovery Procedures

  1. Run full startup procedure including background counts
  2. Run all three levels of QC
  3. Process 5 patient samples and compare to backup method
  4. Clear backlog by priority (STAT → Urgent → Routine)
  5. Document entire event in downtime log
  6. Conduct root cause analysis within 48 hours
