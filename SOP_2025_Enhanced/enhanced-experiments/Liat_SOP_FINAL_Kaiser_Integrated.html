1. PURPOSE

This document describes the standard operating procedures for performing molecular diagnostic testing using the Roche cobas® Liat System at Kaiser Permanente Largo Laboratory. This SOP ensures accurate, reliable, and rapid point-of-care molecular test results for patient care.

Why Liat Molecular Testing Is Important:

The cobas® Liat System provides rapid molecular diagnostic results at the point of care, enabling:

  • Rapid detection of infectious agents (Influenza A/B, RSV, Strep A, SARS-CoV-2)
  • Quick turnaround time (20 minutes or less)
  • Immediate clinical decision making
  • Improved patient outcomes through timely treatment
  • Reduced unnecessary antibiotic use
  • Infection control and isolation decisions

2. SCOPE

This procedure applies to Medical Laboratory Technicians (MLTs), Medical Laboratory Scientists (MLSs), and authorized Point-of-Care personnel trained and competent in performing molecular testing on the cobas® Liat System at Kaiser Permanente Largo Laboratory.

Test Menu Includes:

  • Influenza A/B
  • Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV)
  • Influenza A/B & RSV
  • Strep A
  • SARS-CoV-2 (COVID-19)
  • SARS-CoV-2 & Influenza A/B
  • Clostridium difficile (C. diff)

Personnel Requirements:

Only trained and competency-assessed personnel may operate the cobas® Liat System. Training includes:

  1. Initial training by qualified trainer (minimum 8 hours)
  2. Supervised practice (minimum 10 patient samples)
  3. Written competency assessment
  4. Practical competency demonstration
  5. Annual competency reassessment
  6. Completion of Roche cobas® Liat online training modules

3. RESPONSIBILITIES

Medical Laboratory Technician (MLT)

  • Perform daily QC and document results
  • Process STAT molecular specimens
  • Maintain cobas® Liat cleanliness and perform daily maintenance
  • Report critical/positive results immediately to nursing staff

  • Maintain adequate inventory of test tubes and supplies
  • Monitor test tube expiration dates

Medical Laboratory Scientist (MLS)

  • Perform all MLT duties as needed
  • Review and validate QC results

Bench Lead Technologist

  • Oversee daily operations of molecular testing
  • Review and sign off on monthly QC summaries
  • Coordinate maintenance schedules
  • Investigate QC failures and implement corrective actions
  • Serve as primary contact for technical issues
  • Maintain competency assessment records
  • Update procedures as needed
  • Monitor turnaround times and implement improvements

Clinical Impact

Influenza A/B Testing

Clinical Significance: Enables rapid antiviral treatment within 48 hours of symptom onset.

  • Sensitivity: 97.8% for Influenza A, 98.1% for Influenza B
  • Specificity: 99.5% for both
  • Treatment Impact: Reduces symptom duration by 1-2 days if treated early
  • Public Health: Prevents transmission in healthcare settings

Strep A Testing

Clinical Significance: Prevents complications of untreated streptococcal infection.

  • Sensitivity: 97.4%
  • Specificity: 95.8%
  • Prevents: Rheumatic fever, post-streptococcal glomerulonephritis
  • Antibiotic Stewardship: Reduces unnecessary antibiotic use

SARS-CoV-2 Testing

Clinical Significance: Critical for COVID-19 diagnosis and infection control.

  • Sensitivity: 96.7%
  • Specificity: 99.3%
  • Detection: Can detect virus 1-2 days before symptom onset
  • Variants: Detects all known variants of concern

8. SAFETY REQUIREMENTS

⚠️ Universal Precautions

All patient specimens must be handled as potentially infectious. Follow standard precautions at all times.

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

  • Laboratory coat (must be worn at all times)
  • Disposable gloves (change between specimens)
  • Safety glasses or face shield when handling open specimens
  • N95 respirator when handling suspected COVID-19 specimens (if aerosolization risk)

Specimen Handling

  1. Process all specimens in a biological safety cabinet when possible
  2. Use sealed carriers for specimen transport
  3. Decontaminate work surfaces with 10% bleach or EPA-approved disinfectant
  4. Dispose of all test tubes and swabs in biohazard waste
  5. Wash hands thoroughly after removing gloves

Spill Procedure

Biological Spill Response

  1. Alert others in the area
  2. Put on appropriate PPE
  3. Cover spill with absorbent material
  4. Pour 10% bleach over the spill, working from outside to center
  5. Allow 20 minutes contact time
  6. Clean up with disposable materials
  7. Dispose in biohazard waste
  8. Decontaminate area again
  9. Document incident

Specimen Rejection Criteria

Reject specimens if:

  • Unlabeled or mislabeled
  • Wrong specimen type (e.g., saliva for flu test)
  • Specimen leaked or contaminated
  • Exceeded stability limits
  • Calcium alginate or wooden shaft swabs (inhibit PCR)
  • Dry swabs without transport media

10. QUALITY CONTROL REQUIREMENTS

⚠️ CRITICAL QC RULE

NEVER report patient results if QC is not acceptable. This is a zero-tolerance policy.

QC Schedule

QC Type Frequency Acceptable Criteria
QC Check Tube Daily before patient testing PASS result required
External Controls With each new lot/shipment Positive and negative controls pass
Internal Control With each patient sample IC must be detected

QC Failure Response

QC Failure Actions

  1. DO NOT report any patient results
  2. Repeat QC immediately with new QC tube
  3. If QC fails again:
    • Check expiration dates
    • Check storage conditions
    • Check for error messages
    • Run QC on different Liat instrument if available
  4. If QC still fails:
    • Remove instrument from service
    • Contact Lead Technologist
    • Contact Roche Technical Support if needed
    • Document in instrument log
  5. Do not resume testing until QC passes

13. TEST PROCEDURES

General Testing Procedure

Step-by-Step Testing Process

  1. Specimen Receipt
    • Verify patient identification matches requisition
    • Check specimen adequacy
    • Note collection time
  2. Pre-analytical
    • Allow refrigerated specimens to come to room temperature (15 min)
    • Mix specimen gently by inverting 5 times
    • Remove test tube from pouch
  3. Sample Preparation
    • Using transfer pipette, add sample to fill line on test tube
    • Cap tube securely
    • Invert 3 times to mix
  4. Loading Sample
    • Press "Run Test" on Liat screen
    • Scan or enter patient ID
    • Scan test tube barcode
    • Open drawer when prompted
    • Insert tube with barcode facing out
    • Close drawer
  5. During Run
    • Monitor progress on screen
    • Do not open drawer during run
    • Run time varies by test (15-20 minutes)
  6. Result Review
    • Review result on screen
    • Check for error flags
    • Verify internal control passed
  7. Result Reporting
    • Release result to LIS
    • Call critical/positive results immediately
    • Document in log if required
  8. Post-analytical
    • Remove and dispose test tube in biohazard waste
    • Clean drawer if needed
    • Store specimen per retention policy

Test-Specific Procedures

Strep A Testing

Special Considerations:

  • Throat swab must contact posterior pharynx and both tonsillar pillars
  • Avoid touching tongue, teeth, or gums
  • Process within 4 hours if stored at room temperature
  • Positive results are definitive
  • Negative results in high-risk patients may need backup culture

COVID-19 Testing

Special Considerations:

  • Use enhanced PPE when handling specimens
  • Process in biological safety cabinet if available
  • Report all positives to Infection Control immediately
  • Follow state reporting requirements

17. LIMITATIONS

Test Limitations

  • Results should be interpreted in conjunction with clinical presentation
  • Negative results do not rule out infection
  • Test performance may be affected by:
    • Improper specimen collection
    • Specimen collected too early or late in disease course
    • Improper specimen transport or storage
    • Technical errors in performing the test
    • Viral load below limit of detection
  • Mutations in viral genome may affect detection
  • Does not differentiate between viable and non-viable organisms

Interfering Substances

The following may interfere with test results:

  • Blood contamination (>5%)
  • Mucoid specimens
  • Nasal sprays or drops if used within 24 hours
  • Throat lozenges or mouth rinses

19. DOWNTIME PROCEDURES

⚠️ Instrument Downtime Protocol

When cobas® Liat is unavailable:

Immediate Actions

  1. Notify nursing units and providers
  2. Post downtime notices
  3. Implement alternative testing methods

Alternative Testing Options

Test Alternative Method TAT
Influenza A/B Send to main lab for PCR 2-4 hours
Strep A Rapid antigen test or culture 15 min or 24-48 hrs
SARS-CoV-2 Send to main lab for PCR 2-4 hours
RSV Send to main lab for PCR 2-4 hours

Specimen Handling During Downtime

  • Label specimens clearly with collection time
  • Store at 2-8°C if delay expected
  • Batch specimens for send-out if needed
  • Document all specimens affected
  • Prioritize STAT and critical specimens

Recovery Procedures

  1. When instrument returns to service:
    • Run QC before patient testing
    • Process backlogged STAT specimens first
    • Notify units that testing has resumed
    • Document downtime duration and cause
