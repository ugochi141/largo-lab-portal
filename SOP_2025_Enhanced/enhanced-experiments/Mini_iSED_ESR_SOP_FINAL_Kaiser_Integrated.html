1. PURPOSE

This procedure describes the standardized methodology for performing Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate (ESR) testing using the Mini iSED automated ESR analyzer at Kaiser Permanente Largo Laboratory. The Mini iSED provides accurate, automated ESR measurements in compliance with CLIA, CAP, and Joint Commission requirements.

Key Objectives

  • Standardize ESR testing across all shifts and operators
  • Ensure accurate and timely ESR results for patient care
  • Maintain regulatory compliance with all accrediting bodies
  • Minimize pre-analytical and analytical errors
  • Provide consistent, reproducible results

4. TEST PRINCIPLE

The Mini iSED measures the erythrocyte sedimentation rate using an automated photometric capillary stopped flow kinetic analysis method. The system:

Measurement Process

  1. Aspirates whole blood sample into measurement capillary
  2. Positions sample vertically in temperature-controlled chamber (18-25Â°C)
  3. Uses infrared sensors to track RBC-plasma interface
  4. Measures aggregation and sedimentation kinetics
  5. Calculates ESR value using proprietary algorithms
  6. Corrects for hematocrit variations automatically
  7. Reports results in mm/hour (Westergren equivalent)

Method Correlation: Results correlate with modified Westergren method (rÂ² > 0.95)

5. CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE

ESR is a non-specific marker of inflammation used to:

  • Monitor inflammatory and autoimmune diseases
  • Screen for occult disease
  • Monitor treatment response in temporal arteritis and polymyalgia rheumatica
  • Support diagnosis of multiple myeloma and other plasma cell dyscrasias
  • Assess disease activity in rheumatoid arthritis
Clinical Alert: ESR must be interpreted in conjunction with clinical findings and other laboratory results. Isolated ESR elevation is not diagnostic of any specific condition.

11. PATIENT TESTING PROCEDURE

Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Pre-analytical:
    • Verify patient identification (two identifiers)
    • Check specimen collection time and temperature
    • Inspect for clots, hemolysis, or other issues
    • Allow refrigerated samples to reach room temperature (15 minutes)
  2. Sample Preparation:
    • Mix specimen gently by inversion (8-10 times)
    • Do not vortex or shake vigorously
    • Ensure no bubbles present
  3. Analyzer Operation:
    • Log into Mini iSED system with user ID
    • Verify QC is current and acceptable
    • Select "Patient Test" mode
    • Scan specimen barcode
    • Load specimen in designated position
    • Press "Start" to begin analysis
  4. During Analysis:
    • Monitor for error messages
    • Observe for proper sample aspiration
    • Do not disturb analyzer during measurement
    • Analysis time: approximately 20 seconds
  5. Result Review:
    • Verify patient demographics match
    • Check for analyzer flags or warnings
    • Compare with previous results if available
    • Apply delta checks (>50% change from previous)
  6. Result Release:
    • Approve result in LIS
    • Add comments for abnormal results
    • Notify provider for critical values
Important: Do not report results if:
  • QC is not acceptable
  • Specimen quality is questionable
  • Analyzer displays error codes
  • Results are incompatible with clinical picture

12. RESULTS AND INTERPRETATION

Result Range Interpretation Clinical Correlation
0-20 mm/hr Normal No evidence of inflammation
21-40 mm/hr Mild elevation Minor inflammation, infection
41-70 mm/hr Moderate elevation Active inflammatory process
>70 mm/hr Marked elevation Severe inflammation, possible malignancy
>100 mm/hr Critical elevation Urgent clinical correlation needed

Factors Affecting ESR

Increases ESR: Pregnancy, anemia, renal disease, hyperfibrinogenemia
Decreases ESR: Polycythemia, sickle cells, spherocytosis, hypofibrinogenemia

14. LIMITATIONS

  • Technical Limitations:
    • Cannot process severely lipemic or icteric samples
    • Requires minimum 1.0 mL sample volume
    • Temperature sensitive (must be 18-25Â°C)
    • Cannot analyze clotted specimens
  • Clinical Limitations:
    • Non-specific test - elevated in many conditions
    • Normal ESR does not exclude disease
    • Cannot differentiate between inflammatory conditions
    • Affected by numerous physiological factors
  • Interfering Substances:
    • Heparin contamination causes falsely elevated results
    • Cold agglutinins interfere with measurement
    • Paraproteins may affect sedimentation

20. SAFETY REQUIREMENTS

Biological Safety

  • Treat all specimens as potentially infectious
  • Wear appropriate PPE: gloves, lab coat, safety glasses
  • Work behind splash shield when handling open tubes
  • No eating, drinking, or applying cosmetics in work area
  • Decontaminate work surfaces with 10% bleach or approved disinfectant
  • Dispose of specimens in biohazard containers

Chemical Safety

  • Review SDS for all reagents and cleaning solutions
  • Store chemicals according to compatibility
  • Use fume hood for volatile substances
  • Maintain chemical inventory with expiration dates
  • Report all chemical spills immediately

Emergency Procedures

  • Needlestick/Exposure: Immediately wash area, report to supervisor, seek medical attention
  • Chemical Spill: Contain, neutralize if safe, evacuate if necessary
  • Fire: RACE protocol - Rescue, Alarm, Contain, Evacuate
  • Power Failure: Switch to backup power, implement downtime procedures

22. DOWNTIME PROCEDURES

Immediate Actions During Analyzer Failure

  1. Notify:
    • Lead Technologist immediately
    • Laboratory Supervisor within 30 minutes
    • Affected clinical units within 1 hour
  2. Implement Backup Testing:
    • Route STAT specimens to MOB Laboratory
    • Contact: MOB Lab at 301-555-2000
    • Arrange specimen transport via pneumatic tube or courier
  3. Manual Method (Emergency Only):
    • Use Westergren manual ESR tubes if available
    • Follow manual ESR procedure (separate SOP)
    • Document all manual results with method notation

Extended Downtime Protocol (>4 hours)

  • Activate formal contingency plan with administration
  • Arrange for rental/loaner analyzer if available
  • Consider sending specimens to reference laboratory
  • Update LIS with downtime messages
  • Maintain specimen log for result reconciliation
  • Communicate expected resolution time to clinical units

Recovery Procedures

  1. Perform all required maintenance before restart
  2. Run calibration verification
  3. Analyze QC at all levels (must pass before patient testing)
  4. Process any backlogged STAT specimens first
  5. Verify all results entered correctly in LIS
  6. Document downtime event in quality log
