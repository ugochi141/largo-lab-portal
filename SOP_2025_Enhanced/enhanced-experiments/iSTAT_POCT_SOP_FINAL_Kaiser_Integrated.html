1. PURPOSE

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) provides comprehensive instructions for the operation, maintenance, and quality control of the Abbott iSTAT Point-of-Care Testing System in accordance with Kaiser Permanente Mid-Atlantic States Laboratory standards, ensuring compliance with CLIA, CAP, and Joint Commission requirements.

Key Objectives:
  • Standardize iSTAT testing across all point-of-care locations
  • Ensure accurate and timely critical care testing results
  • Maintain compliance with POCT regulatory requirements
  • Support rapid clinical decision-making at the bedside
  • Minimize turnaround time for critical results
  • Integrate Kaiser Huddle recommendations for quality improvement

üìã Kaiser Huddle Focus

  • iSTAT provides critical results in 2-10 minutes at the point of care
  • Essential for emergency department, ICU, and operating room decisions
  • Reduces need for arterial punctures with capillary blood gas capability
  • Supports sepsis protocols with rapid lactate measurements

2. CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE

The iSTAT System provides rapid, accurate point-of-care testing for critical analytes, enabling immediate clinical interventions in emergency, critical care, and perioperative settings.

5. SAFETY REQUIREMENTS

‚ö†Ô∏è BLOODBORNE PATHOGEN WARNING:

All blood specimens must be handled as potentially infectious. Follow standard precautions and OSHA Bloodborne Pathogen standards at all times.

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

  • Gloves: Required for all testing, change between patients
  • Lab coat or gown: Required in patient care areas
  • Face protection: When splash risk exists
  • Safety glasses: For arterial blood collection

Sharps Safety

  • Use safety-engineered devices for all capillary punctures
  • Activate safety features immediately after use
  • Dispose in approved sharps containers immediately
  • Never recap, bend, or break used lancets
  • Sharps containers must not exceed 3/4 full

Chemical Safety

  • Cartridges contain lithium heparin (anticoagulant)
  • QC materials contain preserved human blood products
  • Calibration verification materials contain synthetic analytes
  • SDS available in POCT areas and online

Electrical Safety

  • Keep analyzers away from fluid sources
  • Use only approved power adapters
  • Report any electrical issues immediately
  • Do not attempt repairs - contact Biomedical Engineering

6. SPECIMEN REQUIREMENTS

Acceptable Specimen Types

Specimen Type Collection Method Volume Required Cartridge Compatibility
Arterial Blood Arterial puncture or line 95 ¬µL minimum All cartridges
Venous Blood Venipuncture or line 95 ¬µL minimum All except blood gas
Capillary Blood Fingerstick/heelstick 95 ¬µL minimum Limited blood gas accuracy
Mixed Venous Central line 95 ¬µL minimum Special interpretation needed

Collection Requirements

  • Anticoagulant: Use balanced lithium heparin only (no sodium heparin)
  • Air bubbles: Must be expelled immediately for blood gas testing
  • Mixing: Roll syringe between palms 5-10 times
  • Testing timeframe: Within 10 minutes of collection for blood gases
  • Temperature: Do not refrigerate or heat specimens

Specimen Rejection Criteria

  • Clotted specimens (visible clots or fibrin)
  • Hemolyzed specimens (may affect K+, AST, LDH)
  • Air bubbles in blood gas specimens
  • Wrong anticoagulant (EDTA, citrate, sodium heparin)
  • Insufficient volume (<95 ¬µL)
  • Specimen age >10 minutes for blood gases
  • Specimen age >30 minutes for other tests

‚ö†Ô∏è Line Draw Precautions

  • Discard 5-10 mL before collecting specimen
  • Turn off IV fluids for 2 minutes before collection
  • Do not collect above IV sites
  • Note any medications that may interfere

13. TEST PROCEDURE

Turnaround Time Requirements

Test Type Analysis Time Total TAT Goal Critical TAT
Blood Gases 2 minutes <15 minutes <10 minutes
Basic Metabolic 2 minutes <15 minutes <10 minutes
Troponin 10 minutes <20 minutes <15 minutes
PT/INR 2 minutes <15 minutes <10 minutes
ACT 6 minutes <10 minutes Immediate

Pre-Analytical Steps

  1. Verify patient identification with two identifiers
  2. Check physician order or protocol
  3. Ensure QC is current (check analyzer screen)
  4. Select appropriate cartridge for ordered tests
  5. Allow cartridge to reach room temperature (minimum 5 minutes)
  6. Check cartridge expiration date

Specimen Collection

  1. Follow standard phlebotomy procedures
  2. Use appropriate collection device:
    • Arterial: ABG syringe with balanced lithium heparin
    • Venous: Lithium heparin tube or syringe
    • Capillary: Heparinized capillary tube
  3. Mix specimen thoroughly (roll between palms)
  4. Expel all air bubbles immediately
  5. Test within time limits (10 min for gases, 30 min for chemistry)

Testing Procedure

  1. Power on analyzer if needed
  2. Enter or scan operator ID
  3. Enter or scan patient ID
  4. Remove cartridge from pouch
  5. Fold down sample well tab
  6. Dispense sample:
    • Fill to fill mark (approximately 95 ¬µL)
    • Avoid air bubbles
    • Do not overfill
  7. Close cartridge immediately
  8. Insert cartridge into analyzer within 5 seconds
  9. Analyzer automatically processes sample
  10. Results display when complete
  11. Review results for critical values
  12. Accept or reject results
  13. Results transmit to LIS automatically
  14. Dispose of cartridge in biohazard waste

Post-Analytical Steps

  1. Review results for critical values
  2. Compare to previous results if available
  3. Document in patient record if at bedside
  4. Notify provider of critical values immediately
  5. Clean analyzer if contaminated
  6. Return analyzer to charging dock

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Testing Notes

  • Never use expired cartridges
  • Do not test clotted specimens
  • Ensure proper patient ID before testing
  • Document any pre-analytical variables
  • Note if patient on oxygen for blood gases

17. LIMITATIONS AND INTERFERENCES

General Limitations

  • Not for use with neonates <48 hours old (bilirubin interference)
  • Capillary blood gas results may differ from arterial
  • Some medications may interfere with specific tests
  • Extreme hematocrit values affect some results
  • Not validated for dialysis patients (some tests)

Known Interferences

Interfering Substance Affected Tests Effect
Hemolysis K+, AST, LDH Falsely elevated
Lipemia Na+, Hgb May be suppressed
High Bilirubin Creatinine Falsely decreased
EDTA contamination iCa, K+ iCa decreased, K+ increased
Sodium heparin Na+, iCa Na+ increased, iCa decreased
Acetaminophen Glucose, Creatinine May interfere at toxic levels
Ascorbic acid Glucose Falsely decreased at high levels

Hematocrit Effects

  • Hct <20%: May affect electrolyte measurements
  • Hct >65%: May cause incomplete filling, affect results
  • Correction factors applied automatically for some tests

18. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

Scenario 1: All iSTAT Analyzers Down

Immediate Actions:

  1. Notify all clinical areas of iSTAT unavailability
  2. Route all specimens to main laboratory
  3. Activate STAT courier service for remote areas
  4. Contact Abbott Technical Support: 1-877-441-7440

Backup Testing Options:

Test Alternative Method Location TAT
Blood Gases Main lab ABG analyzer Core Laboratory 30 minutes
Basic Metabolic Chemistry analyzer Core Laboratory 45 minutes
Troponin Chemistry analyzer Core Laboratory 60 minutes
PT/INR Coagulation analyzer Core Laboratory 45 minutes
Lactate Chemistry analyzer Core Laboratory 30 minutes

Scenario 2: Network/LIS Failure

  1. Continue testing - analyzers store results
  2. Use manual result recording sheets
  3. Assign sequential downtime numbers
  4. Call critical values directly
  5. When connectivity restored:
    • Upload all stored results
    • Match to patient records
    • Verify all critical values reported

Scenario 3: Mass Casualty Event

  1. Activate emergency response protocol
  2. Deploy all available analyzers to ED/Triage
  3. Prioritize:
    • Blood gases for respiratory distress
    • Hemoglobin for trauma/bleeding
    • Basic metabolic for shock
  4. Use abbreviated patient identifiers per disaster protocol
  5. Laboratory provides additional operators
  6. Suspend non-critical testing

19. DOWNTIME PROCEDURES

Analyzer Downtime

  1. Single Analyzer Down:
    • Use alternate analyzer in same area
    • If none available, use nearest department
    • Transport specimens with ice if needed
  2. Department Analyzers Down:
    • Route to nearest functional analyzer
    • Prioritize by clinical urgency
    • Consider main laboratory testing
  3. System-wide Failure:
    • Activate contingency plan
    • All POCT to main laboratory
    • Notify medical staff of delays
    • Consider reference laboratory for non-urgent

LIS/EMR Downtime

Manual Process:

  1. Use paper requisitions (downtime forms)
  2. Continue testing - results stored in analyzer
  3. Record results on downtime log sheets
  4. Use downtime patient labels
  5. Call all results to clinical areas
  6. Fax result sheets to units

Recovery Process:

  1. Upload all stored results from analyzers
  2. Enter manual results into LIS
  3. Verify all critical values were reported
  4. Match downtime numbers to patient records
  5. File downtime logs per policy
  6. Review for any missed results
