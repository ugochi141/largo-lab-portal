1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) provides comprehensive guidelines for the complete analysis of semen specimens including physical characteristics, sperm concentration, motility, morphology, and specialized testing. This procedure encompasses specimen collection, processing, analysis, and interpretation according to World Health Organization (WHO) 2010 guidelines for the evaluation of male fertility and reproductive health.

Scope: This SOP applies to all laboratory personnel performing semen analysis using manual and automated methods within Kaiser Permanente laboratory facilities for fertility evaluation, post-vasectomy monitoring, and reproductive health assessment in males.

4. SAFETY PRECAUTIONS AND HAZARD COMMUNICATION

Biological Safety:

  • Treat all semen specimens as potentially infectious
  • Use standard precautions for all body fluids
  • Wear appropriate PPE: gloves, lab coat, safety glasses
  • Work in biological safety cabinet when required
  • Proper disposal of biohazardous waste
  • Hand hygiene before and after specimen handling
  • No eating, drinking, or smoking in laboratory areas
  • Special attention to hepatitis B and HIV transmission risk

Chemical Safety:

  • Review SDS for all reagents and stains
  • Store chemicals according to manufacturer specifications
  • Use fume hood for volatile substances
  • Emergency procedures readily available
  • Spill response materials accessible
  • Proper disposal of chemical waste

Equipment Safety:

  • Proper microscope handling and maintenance
  • Centrifuge safety and balancing procedures
  • Incubator temperature monitoring
  • Electrical safety for heating equipment

7. SPECIMEN COLLECTION AND HANDLING

Patient Preparation:

  • Abstinence period: 2-7 days (optimal 3-5 days)
  • Avoid: Hot baths, saunas, fever 3 months prior
  • Medications: Report any current medications
  • Lifestyle factors: Avoid excessive alcohol, smoking
  • Collection instructions: Provided in advance

Collection Methods:

  1. Masturbation (Preferred):
    • Private collection room or home collection
    • Sterile, wide-mouth container
    • No lubricants or condoms
    • Complete ejaculate collection essential
  2. Special Collection Condoms:
    • Non-toxic, non-spermicidal
    • Used when masturbation not possible
    • Religious or cultural considerations
  3. Coitus Interruptus:
    • Not recommended due to specimen loss
    • Risk of contamination
    • Incomplete collection

Transport and Timing:

  • Transport temperature: 20-37°C (room temperature to body temperature)
  • Transport time: Within 1 hour of collection
  • Avoid: Temperature extremes during transport
  • Analysis timing: Begin within 1 hour of collection
  • Liquefaction time: Allow 15-60 minutes at 37°C

Specimen Rejection Criteria:

  • Unlabeled or mislabeled containers
  • Collection time >1 hour prior to analysis
  • Incomplete specimen collection
  • Temperature abuse during transport
  • Contaminated specimens
  • Wrong collection container used

8. ANALYTICAL PROCEDURE

Initial Assessment (Macroscopic Examination):

  1. Liquefaction:
    • Normal: Complete liquefaction within 60 minutes
    • Incomplete: Thick gel or clumps present
    • Hyperviscous: Threads >2 cm when dropped
  2. Volume Measurement:
    • Graduated cylinder or pipette
    • Record to nearest 0.1 mL
    • Normal range: ≥1.5 mL (WHO 2010)
  3. pH Assessment:
    • pH strips or pH meter
    • Normal range: 7.2-8.0
    • Low pH may indicate absence of seminal vesicles
  4. Color and Appearance:
    • Normal: Grayish-white, opalescent
    • Yellow: Jaundice, medications, infections
    • Red/brown: Blood contamination
    • Clear: Azoospermia or severe oligozoospermia

Microscopic Examination:

Initial Wet Preparation:

  1. Mix specimen thoroughly
  2. Place 10 μL on glass slide
  3. Cover with 22x22 mm coverslip
  4. Examine within 30 minutes

Sperm Motility Assessment:

Category WHO Classification Description Symbol
Progressive motility Category A Fast progressive PR
Progressive motility Category B Slow progressive PR
Non-progressive motility Category C Non-progressive NP
Immotility Category D Immotile IM

Sperm Concentration Determination:

  1. Dilution Preparation:
    • 1:20 dilution in sodium bicarbonate-formalin
    • Mix thoroughly and load hemocytometer
    • Allow settling for 5 minutes
  2. Counting Procedure:
    • Count sperm in large central squares
    • Count minimum 200 sperm across both chambers
    • Include heads touching upper and left borders
    • Exclude heads touching lower and right borders
  3. Calculation:
    • Concentration = (Total count × dilution factor × 10^6) / (Volume counted)
    • Express in millions per mL

Sperm Morphology Assessment:

  1. Slide Preparation:
    • Make thin smear of neat semen
    • Air dry completely
    • Fix in methanol or formalin
    • Stain with Papanicolaou or Diff-Quik
  2. Strict Criteria Evaluation:
    • Assess 200 sperm under oil immersion (1000x)
    • Head: oval, smooth contour, 5-6 μm length, 2.5-3.5 μm width
    • Acrosome: 40-70% of head area
    • Neck/midpiece: thin, regular, 1.5x head length
    • Tail: uniform thickness, uncoiled, 45 μm length
  3. Classification:
    • Normal: Meets all strict criteria
    • Abnormal: Any deviation from normal
    • Calculate percentage normal forms

10. REFERENCE RANGES AND CRITICAL VALUES

WHO 2010 Reference Values:

Parameter Lower Reference Limit (5th percentile) Units Clinical Significance
Volume 1.5 mL Ejaculatory duct function
Total sperm number 39 million per ejaculate Overall fertility potential
Concentration 15 million/mL Spermatogenesis efficiency
Total motility (PR+NP) 40 % Sperm function
Progressive motility (PR) 32 % Transport capability
Normal morphology 4 % Strict criteria assessment

Clinical Terminology:

Condition Definition Clinical Implication
Normozoospermia All parameters within reference range Normal fertility potential
Oligozoospermia Concentration <15 million/mL Reduced fertility potential
Asthenozoospermia Progressive motility <32% Impaired sperm transport
Teratozoospermia Normal morphology <4% Reduced fertilization capacity
Azoospermia No sperm in ejaculate Requires further evaluation
Cryptozoospermia Sperm found only after centrifugation Severely impaired fertility

Critical Values and Actions:

  • Azoospermia: No sperm detected - requires confirmation
  • Severe oligozoospermia: <5 million/mL - genetic counseling may be indicated
  • Complete asthenozoospermia: No motile sperm - suggests flagellar defects
  • Hematospermia: Blood in semen - rule out malignancy or infection
  • Very low volume: <0.5 mL - possible retrograde ejaculation

11. RESULT INTERPRETATION AND REPORTING

Comprehensive Report Components:

  • Physical characteristics: Volume, pH, appearance, liquefaction
  • Concentration: Million/mL and total count
  • Motility: Progressive, non-progressive, immotile percentages
  • Morphology: Percentage normal forms (strict criteria)
  • Additional findings: Round cells, debris, agglutination
  • Clinical interpretation: Comparison to reference values

Specialized Testing Results:

  • Vitality testing: Percentage live sperm
  • Anti-sperm antibodies: Immunobead or mixed agglutination reaction
  • Biochemical markers: Fructose, zinc, acid phosphatase
  • DNA fragmentation: Sperm chromatin structure assay

Quality Indicators:

  • Total motile sperm count (TMC)
  • Sperm morphology index
  • Multiple anomaly index
  • Teratozoospermia index

13. TROUBLESHOOTING

Common Issues and Solutions:

Problem Possible Cause Solution Prevention
Incomplete liquefaction Infection, enzyme deficiency Mechanical disruption, report finding Proper incubation time
High viscosity Incomplete liquefaction Needle disruption, note in report Extended incubation
Sperm agglutination Anti-sperm antibodies Dilute with medium, perform antibody testing Proper specimen handling
Inconsistent counts Poor mixing, debris Re-mix, re-count, check for clumping Thorough specimen mixing
Rapid motility decline Temperature, pH, contamination Check conditions, analyze quickly Proper temperature control
