1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) provides comprehensive guidelines for the diagnosis of bacterial vaginosis using multiple methodologies including Amsel criteria, Gram stain interpretation (Nugent score), molecular assays, and rapid diagnostic tests. This procedure encompasses specimen collection, processing, testing, and result interpretation for the diagnosis of bacterial vaginosis in women of reproductive age.

Scope: This SOP applies to all laboratory personnel performing bacterial vaginosis testing using microscopic examination, Gram staining, molecular diagnostics, and rapid testing methods within Kaiser Permanente laboratory facilities for women presenting with vaginal symptoms or during routine gynecological examinations.

7. SPECIMEN COLLECTION AND HANDLING

Specimen Types:

  • Vaginal swabs: Preferred for all testing methods
  • Vaginal fluid: Direct collection during examination
  • Self-collected swabs: Alternative for molecular testing
  • Clinician-collected swabs: Gold standard for microscopy

Collection Technique:

  1. Patient Preparation:
    • No douching 24 hours before collection
    • No vaginal medications 24 hours before collection
    • No sexual intercourse 24 hours before collection
    • Not during menstruation (if possible)
  2. Collection Procedure:
    • Insert speculum without lubricant
    • Visualize vaginal walls and cervix
    • Collect specimen from lateral vaginal walls
    • Avoid cervical mucus and blood
    • Use gentle rotation to collect adequate material

Multiple Test Collection:

  • Collect multiple swabs if various tests ordered
  • Use appropriate transport medium for each test
  • Label each specimen with test type
  • Maintain proper collection order
  • Consider self-collection for molecular tests

Transport and Storage:

Test Method Transport Medium Storage Temperature Maximum Time
Wet mount/pH Saline or dry swab Room temperature 1 hour (immediate preferred)
Gram stain Dry swab Room temperature 24 hours
Rapid test Per manufacturer Room temperature 4 hours
Molecular testing Specific transport medium 2-30°C 14-30 days

Rejection Criteria:

  • Unlabeled or mislabeled specimens
  • Insufficient specimen volume
  • Contaminated specimens (fecal, blood)
  • Inappropriate transport medium
  • Specimens exceeding stability limits
  • Cervical specimens (when vaginal requested)

8. ANALYTICAL PROCEDURE

Clinical Diagnostic Criteria (Amsel Criteria):

Bacterial vaginosis diagnosed when 3 of 4 criteria present:

  1. Vaginal pH >4.5
  2. Positive "whiff test" (fishy odor with 10% KOH)
  3. Clue cells >20% of epithelial cells
  4. Thin, homogeneous vaginal discharge

Wet Mount Examination:

  1. Saline Preparation:
    • Place small amount of specimen on slide
    • Add drop of normal saline
    • Cover with coverslip
    • Examine under 10x and 40x magnification
  2. KOH Preparation:
    • Place specimen on separate slide
    • Add drop of 10% KOH
    • Note any fishy odor (whiff test)
    • Examine for yeast forms
  3. Clue Cell Assessment:
    • Identify epithelial cells with adherent bacteria
    • Obscured cell borders characteristic
    • Count percentage of clue cells
    • >20% indicative of bacterial vaginosis

Gram Stain Procedure (Nugent Score):

  1. Slide Preparation:
    • Roll swab on glass slide
    • Heat fix or air dry
    • Label slide appropriately
  2. Staining Steps:
    • Crystal violet - 1 minute
    • Rinse with water
    • Iodine solution - 1 minute
    • Rinse with water
    • Decolorize with acetone-alcohol
    • Rinse immediately
    • Safranin counterstain - 30 seconds
    • Rinse and air dry
  3. Microscopic Examination:
    • Examine under oil immersion (1000x)
    • Score 10-20 fields
    • Count bacterial morphotypes
    • Calculate Nugent score

Nugent Scoring System:

Morphotype 0 Points 1 Point 2 Points 3 Points 4 Points
Large Gram+ rods (Lactobacilli) ≥30 5-30 1-4 <1 0
Small Gram- rods (Gardnerella/Bacteroides) 0 <1 1-4 5-30 ≥30
Curved Gram- rods (Mobiluncus) 0 <5 ≥5 N/A N/A

Nugent Score Interpretation:

  • 0-3: Normal vaginal flora
  • 4-6: Intermediate flora
  • 7-10: Bacterial vaginosis

Stain Quality Control:

    • Staphylococcus epidermidis (Gram-positive)
    • Escherichia coli (Gram-negative)
    • Mixed control with both organisms
  • Acceptance criteria:
    • Clear color differentiation
    • No over-decolorization
    • Appropriate background staining

Clinical Considerations:

  • Pregnancy: BV associated with preterm labor risk
  • Surgery: BV increases post-operative infection risk
  • STI risk: BV increases susceptibility to other infections
  • Recurrent BV: May require extended or alternative treatment
  • Asymptomatic BV: Treatment recommendations vary by patient population

11. RESULT INTERPRETATION AND REPORTING

Reporting Categories:

  • Positive for BV: Meets diagnostic criteria
  • Negative for BV: Normal vaginal flora
  • Intermediate: Mixed or transitional flora
  • Insufficient: Inadequate specimen for evaluation

Amsel Criteria Reporting:

  • List individual criteria met (e.g., "3 of 4 Amsel criteria positive")
  • Specify pH value if measured
  • Note percentage of clue cells observed
  • Indicate whiff test result

Nugent Score Reporting:

  • Report numerical score (0-10)
  • Provide interpretation category
  • Include morphotype distribution when relevant
  • Note specimen adequacy

Additional Findings:

  • Presence of yeast/pseudohyphae
  • Trichomonas organisms
  • Increased white blood cells
  • Other bacterial morphotypes
  • Epithelial cell adequacy
