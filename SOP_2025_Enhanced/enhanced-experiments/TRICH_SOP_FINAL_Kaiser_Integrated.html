1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) provides comprehensive guidelines for the detection and identification of Trichomonas vaginalis using multiple methodologies including wet mount microscopy, antigen detection assays, nucleic acid amplification tests (NAATs), and culture methods. This procedure encompasses specimen collection, processing, testing, and result interpretation for the diagnosis of trichomoniasis.

Scope: This SOP applies to all laboratory personnel performing Trichomonas vaginalis testing using microscopic examination, rapid antigen tests, molecular assays, and culture methods within Kaiser Permanente laboratory facilities for patients of all ages with suspected trichomoniasis.

7. SPECIMEN COLLECTION AND HANDLING

Acceptable Specimens:

  • Vaginal swabs: Preferred for females
  • Cervical swabs: During pelvic examination
  • Urethral swabs: For males (less common)
  • First-void urine: Alternative for males and females
  • Vaginal secretions: Self-collected or provider-collected

Collection Requirements:

  • Use appropriate swab type (polyester or rayon)
  • Collect adequate specimen volume
  • Avoid lubricants and antiseptics
  • Label with patient identification and collection time
  • Transport in appropriate medium if required
  • Minimum 30 mL first-void urine for molecular testing

8. ANALYTICAL PROCEDURE

Wet Mount Microscopy:

  1. Preparation:
    • Place small drop of specimen on glass slide
    • Add drop of normal saline if specimen is dry
    • Cover with coverslip
    • Examine within 10 minutes of preparation
  2. Microscopic Examination:
    • Use 10x objective for initial scanning
    • Switch to 40x objective for detailed examination
    • Look for motile, pear-shaped organisms
    • Observe characteristic jerky, tumbling motility
    • Size: 7-23 Î¼m in length
  3. Confirmatory Features:
    • Undulating membrane
    • Four anterior flagella
    • Prominent nucleus
    • Axostyle extending from posterior end

Rapid Antigen Test (OSOM):

  1. Bring all components to room temperature
  2. Add 3 drops of sample buffer to specimen
  3. Mix thoroughly and wait 1 minute
  4. Transfer entire mixture to test device
  5. Read results at exactly 10 minutes
  6. Do not read after 20 minutes

Molecular Testing (Aptima TV):

  1. Collect specimen in Aptima transport tube
  2. Process according to manufacturer's protocol
  3. Load specimens onto automated platform
  4. Review results and quality control
  5. Interpret based on relative light units (RLU)

Culture Method (InPouch TV):

  1. Inoculate specimen into InPouch TV medium
  2. Incubate at 35-37Â°C in anaerobic conditions
  3. Examine daily for up to 5 days
  4. Look for motile trichomonads
  5. Confirm with microscopic examination

Critical Values and Reporting:

  • Reporting requirement: Follow state/local STI reporting guidelines
  • Contact tracing: Partner notification recommended
  • Treatment urgency: Immediate treatment indicated
  • Pregnancy considerations: Special treatment protocols

11. RESULT INTERPRETATION AND REPORTING

Interpretation Guidelines:

  • Positive: Trichomonas vaginalis detected
  • Negative: Trichomonas vaginalis not detected
  • Equivocal: Repeat testing recommended (molecular only)
  • Invalid: Test failure, repeat required

Clinical Correlations:

  • Wet mount sensitivity: 60-70% (specificity >95%)
  • Rapid antigen sensitivity: 83-98% (specificity >95%)
  • Molecular sensitivity: >95% (specificity >98%)
  • Culture sensitivity: 95% (specificity 100%)

Reporting Format:

  • Method used for detection
  • Qualitative result (positive/negative)
  • Reference ranges and methodology
  • Comment on treatment recommendations if positive
