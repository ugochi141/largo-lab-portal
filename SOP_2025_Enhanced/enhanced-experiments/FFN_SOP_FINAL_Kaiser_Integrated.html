1. PURPOSE

This procedure describes the standardized methodology for performing fetal fibronectin testing at Kaiser Permanente Largo Laboratory. The fFN test aids in assessing the risk of preterm delivery in symptomatic pregnant women between 22-34 weeks gestation, supporting clinical decision-making for patient management.

Key Objectives

  • Provide rapid, accurate fFN testing for preterm labor risk assessment
  • Support clinical decisions regarding hospital admission and treatment
  • Maintain high negative predictive value (>99% for delivery within 7 days)
  • Ensure proper specimen collection and handling
  • Meet urgent turnaround time requirements for L&D patients

4. TEST PRINCIPLE

The Rapid fFN test is a qualitative immunoassay detecting fetal fibronectin in cervicovaginal secretions:

  • Lateral flow immunochromatographic device
  • Monoclonal antibodies specific for fetal fibronectin
  • Detection threshold: 50 ng/mL
  • Visual interpretation of test and control lines
  • Results in 10 minutes

Clinical Utility

  • Negative Result: <1% chance of delivery within 7-14 days
  • Positive Result: Increased risk of preterm delivery
  • Most valuable for negative predictive value
  • Helps avoid unnecessary interventions

6. SPECIMEN REQUIREMENTS

Critical Collection Requirements

  • Timing: 22 weeks 0 days to 34 weeks 6 days gestation
  • Collection: BEFORE digital exam or transvaginal ultrasound
  • Method: Sterile speculum exam by trained provider
  • Site: Posterior fornix of vagina or ectocervix
  • Device: Use ONLY provided polyester swab
  • Duration: Gentle rotation for 10 seconds

Specimen REJECTION Criteria

  • Collection after digital cervical exam
  • Recent intercourse (<24 hours)
  • Vaginal bleeding (moderate to heavy)
  • Ruptured membranes
  • Cervical cerclage in place
  • Advanced cervical dilation (>3 cm)
  • Wrong swab type used
Parameter Requirement Comments
Swab Type Kit-provided polyester only Cotton swabs invalid
Transport Room temperature In provided buffer tube
Stability 8 hours in buffer Test immediately if possible
Volume Adequate saturation 10-second collection

11. TEST PROCEDURES

Step-by-Step Testing Procedure

  1. Pre-analytical:
    • Verify patient meets testing criteria
    • Check specimen collection requirements met
    • Allow test cassette to reach room temperature (15 min)
    • Label cassette with patient ID
  2. Sample Preparation:
    • Mix specimen in buffer tube gently
    • Let stand 10 seconds for complete elution
    • Do not centrifuge or filter
  3. Test Performance:
    • Remove cassette from pouch immediately before use
    • Place on flat surface
    • Add exactly 200 µL specimen to sample well
    • Start timer immediately
    • Read results at exactly 10 minutes
    • Do NOT read after 20 minutes
  4. Result Interpretation:
    • Negative: Control line only
    • Positive: Both control and test lines (any intensity)
    • Invalid: No control line - repeat test
Critical Points:
  • Read at EXACTLY 10 minutes - not before or after
  • Any visible test line = positive (even if faint)
  • Invalid results must be repeated with new cassette

12. RESULTS AND INTERPRETATION

Result Interpretation Guide

Positive ~20% delivery within 7 days PPV: 16-20%
Positive ~30% delivery within 14 days PPV: 25-30%
Reporting Comments:
• Negative: "fFN negative - Low risk of preterm delivery within 7-14 days"
• Positive: "fFN positive - Increased risk of preterm delivery"
• Always include collection time and gestational age

14. LIMITATIONS

  • Not validated for multiple gestations
  • False positives with blood contamination
  • False positives with recent intercourse
  • False positives with bacterial vaginosis
  • Cannot predict exact timing of delivery
  • Lower PPV than NPV
  • Not useful after membrane rupture
  • Performance affected by cervical manipulation

15. CRITICAL VALUES

No critical values defined. All positive results should be communicated promptly to L&D for clinical correlation.

Result Communication:

• Document notification in patient record
