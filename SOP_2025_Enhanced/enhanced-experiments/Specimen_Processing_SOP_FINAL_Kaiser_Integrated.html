1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) provides comprehensive guidelines for the proper receipt, processing, handling, storage, and distribution of clinical specimens. This procedure encompasses all pre-analytical processes from specimen receipt through preparation for testing, ensuring specimen integrity and accurate test results.

Scope: This SOP applies to all laboratory personnel involved in specimen processing including accessioning staff, medical laboratory technicians, and supervisory personnel within Kaiser Permanente laboratory facilities handling blood, urine, body fluids, and tissue specimens.

4. SAFETY PRECAUTIONS AND HAZARD COMMUNICATION

Biological Safety:

  • Treat all specimens as potentially infectious
  • Use standard precautions for all body fluids
  • Wear appropriate PPE: gloves, lab coat, eye protection
  • Work in biological safety cabinet when required
  • Proper disposal of biohazardous waste
  • Hand hygiene before and after specimen handling
  • Immunization against hepatitis B for all personnel

Physical Safety:

  • Safe operation of centrifuges and processors
  • Proper lifting techniques for heavy specimens
  • Slip-resistant flooring in wet areas
  • Emergency eyewash and shower stations accessible
  • Fire safety and evacuation procedures

Chemical Safety:

  • Review SDS for all chemicals and preservatives
  • Proper storage of reagents and specimens
  • Spill response procedures and cleanup materials
  • Fume hood use for volatile substances

7. SPECIMEN COLLECTION AND HANDLING

Specimen Receipt Process:

  1. Initial Assessment:
    • Verify requisition completeness
    • Check specimen integrity and condition
    • Assess temperature requirements
    • Identify priority level (STAT, urgent, routine)
  2. Identification Verification:
    • Match specimen label to requisition
    • Verify patient demographics
    • Check collection date and time
    • Confirm test orders and special requirements
  3. Specimen Logging:
    • Scan barcode or manually enter specimen ID
    • Record receipt time in LIS
    • Document any special conditions or observations
    • Assign laboratory accession number

Rejection Criteria:

  • Unlabeled or mislabeled specimens
  • Requisition and specimen ID mismatch
  • Insufficient volume for requested tests
  • Hemolyzed specimens (when inappropriate)
  • Clotted anticoagulated specimens
  • Contaminated specimens
  • Specimens with compromised container integrity
  • Expired or deteriorated specimens
  • Inappropriate transport conditions

8. ANALYTICAL PROCEDURE

Processing Workflow by Specimen Type:

Blood Specimens:

  1. Serum Specimens:
    • Allow clotting for 30-60 minutes at room temperature
    • Centrifuge at 1000-1300 x g for 10-15 minutes
    • Separate serum into labeled aliquot tubes
    • Cap and store according to test requirements
  2. Plasma Specimens:
    • Verify proper anticoagulant and mixing
    • Centrifuge within specified time limits
    • Separate plasma carefully to avoid cell contamination
    • Aliquot into appropriate storage containers
  3. Whole Blood Specimens:
    • Mix gently to ensure homogeneity
    • Verify adequate volume for testing
    • Maintain at appropriate temperature
    • Process within stability window

Urine Specimens:

  1. Mix specimen gently before processing
  2. Check for adequate volume and clarity
  3. Aliquot for different test requirements
  4. Preserve as needed for specific tests
  5. Label with collection time if timed specimen

Body Fluid Specimens:

  1. Handle with special care due to small volumes
  2. Prioritize testing based on clinical urgency
  3. Coordinate with multiple departments if needed
  4. Document cell count timing requirements
  5. Maintain sterility for culture specimens

10. REFERENCE RANGES AND CRITICAL VALUES

Processing Time Standards:

Priority Level Processing Target Maximum Time Notification Required
STAT 30 minutes 60 minutes Yes, if delayed
Urgent 2 hours 4 hours Yes, if delayed
Routine 4 hours 24 hours No
Send-out 2 hours 4 hours If shipping deadline missed

Temperature Requirements:

Specimen Type Storage Temperature Maximum Time Special Requirements
Routine chemistry 2-8°C 7 days Separate serum/plasma
Hematology Room temperature 24 hours Do not refrigerate
Coagulation Room temperature 4 hours Frozen plasma for special tests
Urinalysis 2-8°C 24 hours Room temperature for 2 hours

Critical Processing Issues:

  • Temperature excursions: Document and assess specimen viability
  • Processing delays: Notify ordering physician for time-sensitive tests
  • Specimen mix-ups: Stop all processing, investigate immediately
  • Container breakage: Assess contamination risk, recollect if necessary
  • Equipment failure: Implement backup procedures, document impact
