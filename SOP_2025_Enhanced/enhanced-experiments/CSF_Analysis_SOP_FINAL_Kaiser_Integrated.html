1. PURPOSE

This procedure establishes standardized protocols for cerebrospinal fluid analysis at Kaiser Permanente Largo Laboratory. CSF analysis is critical for diagnosing meningitis, encephalitis, subarachnoid hemorrhage, multiple sclerosis, and other central nervous system disorders. This document ensures rapid, accurate testing to support urgent clinical decisions.

Critical Objectives

  • Provide immediate CSF analysis for life-threatening conditions
  • Ensure proper specimen handling to maintain cell viability
  • Coordinate testing across multiple laboratory departments
  • Maintain strict TAT requirements for STAT CSF specimens
  • Support diagnosis of infectious and inflammatory CNS conditions
  • Comply with all regulatory requirements for body fluid testing

2. SCOPE

This procedure applies to all personnel involved in CSF testing:

  • Medical Laboratory Scientists performing cell counts and differentials
  • Chemistry technologists performing glucose and protein analysis
  • Microbiology staff performing cultures and Gram stains
  • Laboratory assistants receiving and distributing specimens

4. TEST PRINCIPLES

CSF Test Components

Cell Count and Differential

  • Manual counting using hemocytometer (Neubauer chamber)
  • Undiluted specimen for normal CSF
  • Dilution with acetic acid for high counts
  • Wright-Giemsa stain for differential on concentrated specimen

Chemistry Analysis

  • Glucose: Hexokinase method (compare with serum glucose)
  • Protein: Turbidimetric or dye-binding method
  • Lactate: Enzymatic method (bacterial vs viral differentiation)
  • LDH: Kinetic enzymatic method

Microbiological Studies

  • Gram stain for bacteria
  • India ink or cryptococcal antigen
  • Bacterial culture (aerobic/anaerobic)
  • Viral PCR panels when indicated

Specialized Tests

  • Oligoclonal bands (multiple sclerosis)
  • Cytology for malignant cells
  • Flow cytometry for lymphoma
  • Xanthochromia assessment

6. SPECIMEN REQUIREMENTS

CRITICAL: CSF Specimen Handling

  • Collection: Sterile technique by physician only
  • Tubes: Typically 4 tubes collected in order
  • Priority: ALL CSF specimens are STAT
  • Transport: Hand deliver immediately at room temperature
  • Processing: Begin analysis within 1 hour of collection
  • DO NOT REFRIGERATE: Cells deteriorate rapidly
Tube Number Typical Use Department Minimum Volume
Tube 1 Chemistry (glucose, protein) Chemistry 0.5 mL
Tube 2 Microbiology (culture, Gram stain) Microbiology 1.0 mL
Tube 3 Cell count and differential Hematology 0.5 mL
Tube 4 Hold for additional tests Variable 0.5 mL

Specimen Rejection Criteria

  • Unlabeled specimens (NEVER process unlabeled CSF)
  • Visible contamination
  • Specimen >1 hour old without cell count performed
  • Refrigerated specimen for cell count
  • Clotted specimen

11. CSF TESTING PROCEDURES

A. CSF Cell Count Procedure

  1. Initial Assessment:
    • Note appearance (clear, cloudy, bloody, xanthochromic)
    • If bloody, note if clearing occurs between tubes
    • Process immediately - DO NOT REFRIGERATE
  2. Cell Count - Clear CSF:
    • Mix specimen gently
    • Load both sides of hemocytometer with undiluted CSF
    • Count WBCs in all 9 large squares on both sides
    • Count RBCs in 5 small center squares if present
  3. Cell Count - Cloudy CSF:
    • Dilute with Turk's solution (1:10 or 1:100)
    • Mix and load hemocytometer
    • Count cells and multiply by dilution factor
  4. Calculations:
    • WBC/µL = (cells counted × dilution) / (squares counted × 0.1)
    • RBC/µL = (cells counted × dilution) / (squares counted × 0.02)

B. CSF Differential Procedure

  1. Prepare Concentrated Smear:
    • Add 1 drop 22% albumin to 0.5 mL CSF
    • Cytocentrifuge at 700 rpm for 5 minutes
    • Air dry slides completely
    • Stain with Wright-Giemsa
  2. Perform Differential:
    • Count 100 cells if possible
    • Report percentages of each cell type
    • Note any abnormal cells (malignant, ependymal)
    • Call pathologist for suspicious cells
  3. If <10 cells present:
    • Report actual number seen
    • Note "Insufficient cells for differential"

C. Traumatic Tap Correction

For bloody CSF, correct WBC count using peripheral blood counts:

  • Corrected WBC = Observed WBC - (Blood WBC × CSF RBC / Blood RBC)
  • Add 1 WBC for every 500-1000 RBCs as rule of thumb
  • Note correction on report

Critical Procedures

  • NEVER refrigerate CSF for cell counts
  • ALWAYS process within 1 hour
  • ALWAYS compare glucose with concurrent serum glucose
  • ALWAYS notify immediately for positive Gram stain

12. RESULTS INTERPRETATION

CSF Analysis Patterns

Finding Interpretation Clinical Action
↑ Neutrophils + ↓ Glucose Bacterial meningitis likely Immediate antibiotic therapy
↑ Lymphocytes + Normal Glucose Viral meningitis likely Supportive care
Xanthochromia + RBCs Subarachnoid hemorrhage Neurosurgery consult
↑ Eosinophils Parasitic infection Consider parasites
Malignant cells Meningeal carcinomatosis Oncology consult
Oligoclonal bands Multiple sclerosis Neurology follow-up

CSF/Serum Glucose Ratio

  • Normal: CSF glucose = 60-70% of blood glucose
  • Decreased ratio (<40%): Bacterial, TB, or fungal meningitis
  • Normal ratio: Viral meningitis, other conditions
  • Always obtain concurrent serum glucose for comparison

14. LIMITATIONS

  • Cell Deterioration: WBCs lyse rapidly; 40% loss in 2 hours at room temp
  • Traumatic Tap: Blood contamination affects all parameters
  • Low Cell Counts: Difficult to perform accurate differential
  • Previous Antibiotics: May cause negative cultures despite infection
  • Small Volume: May limit testing options
  • Refrigeration: Destroys cells, invalidates count
  • Hypoglycorrhachia: May occur in non-infectious conditions

Critical Limitations

  • Normal CSF does not rule out all CNS infections
  • Early bacterial meningitis may show normal glucose
  • Viral PCR may be negative early in infection
  • Xanthochromia develops 2-4 hours after SAH

20. SAFETY REQUIREMENTS

CSF Safety Precautions

  • CSF is HIGH RISK - may contain meningitis pathogens
  • ALWAYS work in biosafety cabinet for opens/transfers
  • Use face shield when uncapping tubes
  • Double gloves recommended
  • Decontaminate all surfaces with 10% bleach
  • Autoclave all CSF waste before disposal

Exposure Protocol

  1. Immediately wash/flush affected area
  2. Report to supervisor
  3. Seek medical evaluation at Employee Health
  4. Complete exposure incident report
  5. Follow up per protocol

22. DOWNTIME PROCEDURES

CSF Testing During Downtime

CSF testing CANNOT be delayed - implement immediately:

  1. Cell Count: Continue with manual hemocytometer
  2. Chemistry:
    • Use POCT glucose meter if validated for CSF
    • Send to MOB Lab: 301-555-2000
  3. Gram Stain: Continue manual staining
  4. If Complete Failure:
    • Hand-carry ALL CSF to MOB Laboratory
    • Call ahead: 301-555-2000
    • Send technologist with specimen if possible
Manual Backup Supplies Location:
• Hemocytometers: Hematology drawer 3
• Counting fluid: Refrigerator shelf 2
• Manual stains: Microbiology cabinet
• Emergency kit: Red box by door
