1. PURPOSE

This procedure describes standardized methodologies for performing coagulation testing at Kaiser Permanente Largo Laboratory. It encompasses routine coagulation assays including Prothrombin Time (PT/INR), Activated Partial Thromboplastin Time (aPTT), Fibrinogen, D-Dimer, and specialized coagulation studies to support diagnosis and monitoring of bleeding and thrombotic disorders.

Key Objectives

  • Ensure accurate and timely coagulation test results for patient care
  • Standardize testing procedures across all shifts
  • Maintain compliance with CLIA, CAP, and Joint Commission standards
  • Support anticoagulation therapy monitoring
  • Provide critical results for surgical and emergency care
  • Minimize pre-analytical variables affecting coagulation testing

4. TEST PRINCIPLES

Prothrombin Time (PT/INR)

Principle: Measures extrinsic and common pathway function

  • Thromboplastin reagent initiates clotting via Factor VII
  • Optical detection of fibrin clot formation
  • Results reported in seconds and INR
  • Primary test for warfarin monitoring

Activated Partial Thromboplastin Time (aPTT)

Principle: Measures intrinsic and common pathway function

  • Contact activator and phospholipid initiate clotting
  • Sensitive to factors VIII, IX, XI, XII
  • Used for heparin monitoring and factor deficiencies

Fibrinogen

Principle: Clauss method - thrombin clotting time

  • Excess thrombin converts fibrinogen to fibrin
  • Clotting time inversely proportional to fibrinogen level
  • Critical for bleeding risk assessment

D-Dimer

Principle: Immunoturbidimetric assay

  • Monoclonal antibodies detect fibrin degradation products
  • Marker of fibrinolysis and thrombosis
  • Used for VTE exclusion

6. SPECIMEN REQUIREMENTS

Critical Specimen Requirements

  • Anticoagulant: 3.2% sodium citrate (light blue top) ONLY
  • Fill Volume: MUST fill to line (9:1 blood to citrate ratio)
  • Mixing: Gentle inversion 3-4 times immediately
  • Transport: Room temperature, upright position
  • Processing: Centrifuge within 1 hour of collection
Parameter Requirement Comments
Tube Type 3.2% sodium citrate (blue) 3.8% citrate NOT acceptable
Minimum Volume 90% of tube fill Underfilled tubes alter ratio
Stability - Whole Blood 4 hours at room temp For PT/INR and aPTT
Stability - Plasma 4 hours at room temp 24 hours at 2-8°C if frozen
Hematocrit Adjustment Required if Hct <25% or >55% Adjust citrate volume

Specimen Rejection Criteria

  • Clotted specimens (check with applicator sticks)
  • Hemolyzed samples (may affect optical endpoint)
  • Underfilled tubes (<90% of required volume)
  • Wrong anticoagulant (EDTA, heparin tubes)
  • Specimens >4 hours old at room temperature
  • Visible lipemia or icterus (may interfere)

9. QUALITY CONTROL

Critical QC Rules - NOTIFICATION REQUIRED

  • 1-3s Rule: One control exceeds ±3 SD - STOP testing
  • 2-2s Rule: Two consecutive controls exceed ±2 SD same direction
  • R-4s Rule: Range between controls exceeds 4 SD
  • 4-1s Rule: Four consecutive controls exceed ±1 SD same side
  • 10x Rule: Ten consecutive points on same side of mean

Action Required: Notify Lead Tech immediately, troubleshoot, do not report results

Test Control Levels Frequency Acceptable Range
PT/INR Normal, Abnormal Every 8 hours ±2 SD or ±15%
aPTT Normal, Abnormal, Heparin Every 8 hours ±2 SD or ±15%
Fibrinogen Normal, Abnormal With each run ±2 SD or ±20%
D-Dimer Negative, Positive Daily Per package insert

11. TEST PROCEDURES

PT/INR Procedure

  1. Specimen Preparation:
    • Verify proper specimen (blue top, filled)
    • Check for clots with applicator sticks
    • Centrifuge 3500 rpm × 15 minutes
    • Obtain platelet-poor plasma (<10,000/µL)
  2. Analysis:
    • Load specimen on analyzer
    • Verify patient demographics
    • Select PT/INR test profile
    • Analyzer adds thromboplastin and calcium
    • Optical detection of clot formation
  3. Result Review:
    • Check for analyzer flags
    • Verify result is within linearity
    • Compare with previous results (delta check)
    • Calculate INR = (PT patient/PT normal)^ISI

aPTT Procedure

  1. Pre-analytical:
    • Note if patient on heparin therapy
    • Process within 4 hours of collection
    • Centrifuge immediately if on heparin
  2. Testing:
    • Incubate plasma at 37°C
    • Add aPTT reagent (activator + phospholipid)
    • Incubate 3-5 minutes
    • Add calcium chloride
    • Measure time to clot formation
  3. Heparin Specimens:
    • If >100 seconds, perform 1:1 dilution
    • Report anti-Xa if available
    • Note heparin therapy on report

Critical Specimen: Mixing Study

  1. Mix patient plasma 1:1 with normal pooled plasma
  2. Perform aPTT immediately and after 2-hour incubation
  3. Interpretation:
    • Correction to normal = factor deficiency
    • No correction = inhibitor present
    • Time-dependent inhibitor = Factor VIII inhibitor

12. RESULTS AND INTERPRETATION

Pattern PT/INR aPTT Possible Causes
Isolated PT prolongation ↑ Normal Factor VII deficiency, early warfarin, mild liver disease
Isolated aPTT prolongation Normal ↑ Heparin, hemophilia A/B, lupus anticoagulant
Both prolonged ↑ ↑ Common pathway defects, DIC, severe liver disease
All tests prolonged ↑ ↑ Multiple factor deficiencies, anticoagulation

Therapeutic Ranges

  • Warfarin (most indications): INR 2.0-3.0
  • Warfarin (mechanical valves): INR 2.5-3.5
  • Heparin (therapeutic): aPTT 60-80 seconds (1.5-2.5× baseline)
  • Direct oral anticoagulants: Variable effects on routine tests

14. LIMITATIONS

  • Pre-analytical Variables:
    • Difficult venipuncture may activate coagulation
    • Heparin contamination from lines causes false prolongation
    • High hematocrit (>55%) causes false prolongation
    • Low hematocrit (<25%) causes false shortening
  • Interfering Substances:
    • Direct thrombin inhibitors affect all clot-based tests
    • Factor Xa inhibitors variably affect PT and aPTT
    • Lupus anticoagulants cause aPTT prolongation without bleeding risk
    • High-dose vitamin C may interfere with some methods
  • Clinical Limitations:
    • Normal results don't exclude all bleeding disorders
    • Platelet function defects not detected
    • Von Willebrand disease may have normal screening tests

17. MAINTENANCE

Daily Maintenance

  • Clean sample probes with distilled water
  • Check reagent levels and expiration dates
  • Verify temperature (analyzer 18-25°C)
  • Run startup diagnostics
  • Perform QC before patient testing
  • Clean work surfaces with 10% bleach

Weekly Maintenance

  • Thorough probe cleaning with enzymatic cleaner
  • Clean cuvette area
  • Check and clean barcode reader
  • Review QC trends and Levey-Jennings charts
  • Verify reagent lot numbers in LIS

Monthly Maintenance

  • Replace tubing as needed
  • Clean and calibrate pipettes
  • Temperature verification with certified thermometer
  • Review error logs and troubleshooting history
  • Update software if applicable

20. SAFETY

Safety Requirements

  • Treat all specimens as potentially infectious
  • Wear gloves, lab coat, and face shield for uncapping
  • Use splash shields when processing specimens
  • No mouth pipetting ever
  • Dispose of sharps in appropriate containers
  • Clean spills with 10% bleach solution
  • Report all exposures immediately

Chemical Safety

  • Review SDS for all reagents
  • Store reagents at proper temperature
  • Use fume hood for volatile substances
  • Calcium chloride: Irritant, avoid skin contact
  • Thromboplastin: Biological material, handle with care

22. DOWNTIME PROCEDURES

Analyzer Failure Protocol

  1. Immediate Actions:
    • Switch to backup analyzer if available
    • Notify supervisor and clinicians
    • Triage STAT specimens
  2. Backup Testing:
    • Send STAT PT/INR to MOB Lab: 301-555-2000
    • Use manual tilt-tube method for emergency
    • POCT INR for critical patients
  3. Extended Downtime (>4 hours):
    • Route all specimens to MOB Laboratory
    • Consider reference lab for specialized tests
    • Maintain specimen log for reconciliation
  4. Recovery:
    • Run QC before resuming testing
    • Process backlog by priority
    • Verify all results entered correctly
Manual PT Method (Emergency Only):
1. Water bath at 37°C
2. Add 0.1 mL plasma + 0.1 mL thromboplastin
3. Incubate 3 minutes
4. Add 0.1 mL calcium chloride
5. Tilt tube, record time to clot
